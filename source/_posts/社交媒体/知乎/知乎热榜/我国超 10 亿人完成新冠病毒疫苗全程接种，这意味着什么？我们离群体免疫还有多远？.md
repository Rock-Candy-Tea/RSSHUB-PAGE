Dav的回答<br><br><p></p><p>忘掉群体免疫这个名词吧。</p><p>有个名词叫<a href="https://www.zhihu.com/search?q=%E7%AA%81%E7%A0%B4%E6%80%A7%E6%84%9F%E6%9F%93&search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_source=Entity&hybrid_search_extra=%7B%22sourceType%22%3A%22answer%22%2C%22sourceId%22%3A2107038001%7D" class="internal">突破性感染</a>，英文breakthrough infection，就是指那些完成疫苗接种超过14天后新冠病毒检测结果仍呈阳性。</p><p>cdc的说法就是：COVID-19 vaccines are effective at preventing infection, serious illness, and death. Most people who get COVID-19 are unvaccinated. However, since vaccines are not 100% effective at preventing infection, some people who are <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/vaccines/fully-vaccinated.html" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">fully vaccinated </a>will still get COVID-19. An infection of a fully vaccinated person is referred to as a “breakthrough infection.”</p><p>早在今年5月，美国疾病控制与预防中心（CDC）就发布报告，在美国超过1.3亿完全接种新冠疫苗的人中，至少出现了10262例新冠肺炎的突破性感染病例。</p><p>而最新的数据，截止到8月31日，cdc收到了全美49个州和地区，共12908名突破性感染患者住院或死亡的报告。</p><figure data-size="normal"><img src="https://pic4.zhimg.com/v2-e96870f7fe102c0cbe1964c86258c5c3_1440w.jpg" data-rawwidth="720" data-rawheight="529" data-size="normal" data-default-watermark-src="https://pic1.zhimg.com/v2-9c04734e49854162f00e502cbf84eeb4_720w.jpg" class="origin_image zh-lightbox-thumb" data-original="https://pic4.zhimg.com/v2-e96870f7fe102c0cbe1964c86258c5c3_r.jpg" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"></figure><p>所以，目前宣传接种疫苗更多的是防重症，防死亡。据<a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//baike.baidu.com/item/%25E4%25B8%25AD%25E5%259B%25BD%25E7%2596%25BE%25E6%258E%25A7%25E4%25B8%25AD%25E5%25BF%2583/8977473" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">中国疾控中心</a>研究员、科研攻关组疫苗研发专班专家组成员<a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//baike.baidu.com/item/%25E9%2582%25B5%25E4%25B8%2580%25E9%25B8%25A3/21176" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">邵一鸣</a>表示，疫苗的防护作用分为三个层面，预防感染是一级防护，这是最高、最理想的目标；预防发病、减轻疾病的严重程度和避免死亡是二级防护；预防接种疫苗再感染的人把病毒传给他人，是三级防护。</p><p>据美国cdc的说法，</p><p>COVID-19 vaccines protect people against severe illness, including disease caused by Delta and other variants circulating in the U.S.</p><ul><li>COVID-19 vaccines protect people from getting infected and severely ill, and significantly reduce the likelihood of hospitalization and death.</li><li>The best way to slow the spread of COVID-19 and to prevent infection by Delta or other variants is to get vaccinated.</li><li>For people who are vaccinated and still get infected (i.e., “breakthrough infections”), there is a risk of transmission to others.</li><li>That is why, if you are vaccinated or unvaccinated and live or work in an area with <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//covid.cdc.gov/covid-data-tracker/%23county-view" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">substantial or high transmission</a> of COVID-19, you – as well as your family and community – will be better protected if you wear a mask when you are in indoor public places.</li><li>People who are <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/vaccines/fully-vaccinated-guidance.html" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">immunocompromised</a> may not always build adequate levels of protection after an initial 2-dose primary mRNA COVID-19 vaccine series. They should continue to take all precautions recommended for unvaccinated people, until advised otherwise by their healthcare provider. Further, CDC recommends that moderately to severely <a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//www.cdc.gov/vaccines/covid-19/clinical-considerations/immunocompromised-patients.html" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">immunocompromised people receive an additional dose</a>.</li></ul><p>上述英文很简单，就不翻译了，意思跟前面提到的我国专家<a href="http://link.zhihu.com/?target=https%3A//baike.baidu.com/item/%25E9%2582%25B5%25E4%25B8%2580%25E9%25B8%25A3/21176" class=" wrap external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">邵一鸣</a>的说法差不多。</p><p>总之，就目前来看，接种比例再高也形成不了群体免疫。短期内，我们回不到新冠以前的生活了。</p>
