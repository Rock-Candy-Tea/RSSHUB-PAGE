<blockquote><p>开个新坑</p></blockquote><img src="https://cors.zfour.workers.dev/?http://img.jandan.net/news/2022/08/f25acb2d91005678edd2b94b17dfa72e.jpg!custom" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"><blockquote><p>
<strong>当我看见树上刻着的情侣名字时，我不会觉得这样很可爱或者浪漫什么的。我只会觉得很诡异，到底有多少人在约会时身上还带着刀？</strong><br>
<em>When I see the names of lovers engraved on a tree, I don't find it cute or romantic. I find it weird how many people take knives with them on dates.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>我和老公做出了一个艰难的决定：我们不打算要孩子了。有想要孩子的，请留下你的联系方式，我们明天就能把孩子送上门。</strong><br>
<em>My husband and I have come to a difficult decision: we don't want children. If anybody does, please leave your contact information, and we'll drop them off tomorrow.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>你们知道有一句谚语叫“一个人的垃圾是另一个人的宝贝”吗？我也不知道为什么，但是被领养的人不喜欢这句话。</strong><br>
<em>Do you know the phrase “One man's trash is another man’s treasure”? I don't know why, but adopted people don't like it.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>我喜欢拿孤儿开玩笑。他们能怎么办？告诉他们的父母吗？</strong><br>
<em> I love telling jokes about orphans. What are they going to do? Tell their parents?</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>牧师问电椅上的死刑犯说：“你还有什么最后的请求吗？”“有的，”死刑犯说，“可以请您握着我的手吗？”</strong><br>
<em> A priest asks the convicted murderer at the electric chair, “Do you have any last requests?” “Yes,” replies the murderer. “Can you please hold my hand?”</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>我有一具完美的胴体，但它在我家的冰柜里。</strong><br>
<em> I’ve got the perfect body, but it’s at home in my freezer.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>女儿问我星星(明星)是怎么死的。“通常是吸毒过量，”我跟她说。</strong><br>
<em> My daughter asked me how stars die. “Usually an overdose,” I told her.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>一个男人从昏迷中醒来。他的妻子换下身上的黑色衣服，怒道：“真就是什么都指不上你，对不对！”</strong><br>
<em> A man wakes from a coma. His wife changes out of her black clothes and, irritated, remarks, “I really cannot depend on you in anything, can I!”</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>我的Frank叔叔去世的时候，想要把自己的骨灰放在最爱的啤酒杯里再埋进地下。他的遗愿是成为“弗兰肯斯坦”。</strong><br>
<em>When my Uncle Frank died, he wanted his cremations to be buried in his favorite beer mug. His last wish was to be Frank in Stein.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>我希望去世的时候能像祖父那样，在睡梦中安然离世。而不像他车里的乘客那样尖叫不止。</strong><br>
<em>When I die, I want to die like my grandfather, who died peacefully in his sleep. Not screaming like all the passengers in his car.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>我恨双标。烧一具尸体，在火葬场烧就是“满怀敬意的朋友。”在家里烧就成了“破坏证据”。</strong><br>
<em>I hate double standards. Burn a body at a crematorium, you're "being a respectful friend." Do it at home and you're "destroying evidence."</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>我从家里的花园挖出来了一个满是金币的箱子。我想立马跑回家，把这事儿告诉妻子。然后我想起来为什么要在花园里挖坑了。</strong><br>
<em>I was digging in our garden and found a chest full of gold coins. I wanted to run straight home to tell my wife about it. Then I remembered why I was digging in our garden.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>我有一个关于涓流经济的笑话，但是99%的人永远都听不懂(得不到)。</strong><br>
<em>I have a joke about trickle-down economics. But 99% of you will never get it.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>Z世代应该改名叫隔离代。</strong><br>
<em>Gen Z should change their name to quaranteens.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>医生：“很快你就能归于平静了。”<br>
男子：“我是要死了吗?”<br>
医生：“不是，但你老婆快了。”</strong><br>
<em> Doctor: “You'll be at peace soon”<br>
Man: “Am I dying?”<br>
Doctor:"No, your wife is"</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>性别就像双子塔。原来只有两个，但现在成了敏感话题。</strong><br>
<em>Genders are like the twin towers. There used to be two of them and now it’s a sensitive subject.</em>
</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>
<strong>“妈，我书包怎么这么沉？”“真主至高伟大，儿子，安拉胡阿克巴。”</strong><br>
<em>“Mom, why is my backpack so heavy? ”“Allahu Akbar my son. Allahu Akbar.”</em>
</p></blockquote>
