font-family: 宋体;">空气污染物中发现的微小可吸入细颗粒物</span>(<a href="https://scitechdaily.com/scientists-discover-how-air-pollution-alters-lung-tissue-increasing-cancer-susceptibility/" target="_self">fine particulate matter</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>FPM)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">已被认定为第一类致癌物，对全球健康构成重大威胁。然而，</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的致癌机制尚不清楚。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">南京大学生命科学学院药物生物技术国家重点实验室（</span>State Key Laboratory of Pharmaceutical Biotechnology, School of Life Sciences, Nanjing University, Nanjing, China<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的副研究员、上述论文的第一作者王珍珍（</span>Zhenzhen Wang<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）解释道：“尽管</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">有可能导致突变，但最近的研究表明，</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">并不直接促进——甚至可能抑制——肺癌细胞的生长。这表明</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">可能通过支持肿瘤生长的间接手段导致癌症。例如，一些研究表明</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">可以阻止免疫细胞移动到需要它们的地方。”王珍珍是南京大学</span>(Nanjing University<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>NJU)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的副研究员（</span>associate researcher<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），在澳门大学奖学金的资助下，她在南京大学和澳门大学的实验室之间进行了这项研究。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的肺癌细胞后，</span>CTLs<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">被招募到肺中破坏肿瘤细胞。相比之下，在肺暴露于</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的小鼠中，</span>CTLs<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的浸润被延迟了——这可能允许肿瘤细胞在肺组织中形成。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">为了研究为什么</span>CTLs<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在暴露于</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的肺中没有那么快进入肺，研究小组研究了</span>CTLs<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">本身和肺组织结构。他们发现暴露于</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的</span>CTLs<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">仍然保持其迁移能力，但暴露于</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">极大地压缩了肺组织结构和免疫细胞在其中移动的空间。胶原蛋白水平也高得多，胶原蛋白是一种为细胞和组织提供生物力学支持的蛋白质。当研究小组在暴露于</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的小鼠肺组织中研究</span>CTLs<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的运动时，</span>CTLs<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">很难移动，而未处理的组织中的</span>CTLs<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">可以自由移动。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">对该组织的进一步分析表明，结构变化是由胶原蛋白亚型胶原</span>IV<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/scientists-discover-how-air-pollution-alters-lung-tissue-increasing-cancer-susceptibility/" target="_self">collagen IV</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的增加引起的，但研究小组仍然不知道</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">是如何引发这一变化的。当他们更仔细地观察</span>IV<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">型胶原蛋白的结构变化以及产生这些变化的酶——过氧蛋白（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/scientists-discover-how-air-pollution-alters-lung-tissue-increasing-cancer-susceptibility/" target="_self">peroxidasin</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）时，他们找到了答案。这种酶驱动一种特定类型的交联，接触</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">被发现会导致肺部组织恶化。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">王珍珍说：“最令人惊讶的发现是这一过程发生的机制。过氧蛋白酶（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/scientists-discover-how-air-pollution-alters-lung-tissue-increasing-cancer-susceptibility/" target="_self">peroxidasin enzyme</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）粘在肺中的</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">上，增加了它的活性。总之，这意味着无论</span>FPM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">进入肺的哪个部位，过氧蛋白酶活性的增加都会导致肺组织的结构变化，从而将免疫细胞拒之门外，使其远离生长中的肿瘤细胞。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述论文的共同通讯作者之一、南京大学生命科学学院教授</span><a href="https://www.baidu.com/link?url=K_VWTJfpOaWFzUB6jEnIZgG7ZuOG6vqun0WnSTFOswMI4PIVw-BuXKAPiji-6_BBpS8mKMyGw9QVNBvsIYOX65fwcTcvoZ9XFyhdiqjFtRK&wd=&eqid=e437af6e0005fdb5000000056264a96b" target="_blank" style="text-decoration: underline; font-family: 宋体;">（</span>Lei Dong<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）总结道：“我们的研究揭示了吸入细颗粒物促进肺肿瘤发展的一种全新机制。我们提供的直接证据表明，粘附在细颗粒物上的蛋白质可以造成重大的不利影响，引发致病活性。我们发现过氧蛋白是肺组织中这种作用的中介，这确定了它是预防空气污染引起的肺部疾病的一个特定的和意想不到的目标。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde"; text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank"><span style="font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Although fine particulate matter (FPM) in air pollutants and tobacco smoke is recognized as a strong carcinogen and global threat to public health, its biological mechanism for inducing lung cancer remains unclear. Here, by investigating FPM's bioactivities in lung carcinoma mice models, we discover that these particles promote lung tumor progression by inducing aberrant thickening of tissue matrix and hampering migration of anti-tumor immunocytes. Upon inhalation into lung tissue, these FPM particles abundantly adsorb peroxidasin (PXDN) - an enzyme mediating type IV collagen (Col IV) crosslinking - onto their surface. The adsorbed PXDN exerts abnormally high activity to crosslink Col IV via increasing the formation of sulfilimine bonds at the NC1 domain, leading to an overly dense matrix in the lung tissue. This disordered structure decreases the mobility of cytotoxic CD8+ T lymphocytes into the lung and consequently impairs the local immune surveillance, enabling the flourishing of nascent tumor cells. Meanwhile, inhibiting the activity of PXDN abolishes the tumor-promoting effect of FPM, indicating the key impact of aberrant PXDN activity on the tumorigenic process. In summary, our finding elucidates a new mechanism for FPM-induced lung tumorigenesis and identifies PXDN as a potential target for treatment or prevention of the FPM-relevant biological risks.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
