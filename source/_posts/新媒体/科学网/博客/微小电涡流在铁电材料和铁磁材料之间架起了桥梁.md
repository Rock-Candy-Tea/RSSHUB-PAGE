font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Ferroics, especially ferromagnets, can form complex topological spin structures such as vortices<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR1" title="Wachowiak, A. et al. Direct observation of internal spin structure of magnetic vortex cores. Science 298, 577–580 (2002)." target="_blank">1</a></sup> and skyrmions<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR2" title="Seki, S., Yu, X. Z., Ishiwata, S. & Tokura, Y. Observation of skyrmions in a multiferroic material. Science 336, 198–201 (2012)." target="_blank">2</a>,<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR3" title="Yu, X. Z. et al. Real-space observation of a two-dimensional skyrmion crystal. Nature 465, 901–904 (2010)." target="_blank">3</a></sup> when subjected to particular electrical and mechanical boundary conditions. Simple vortex-like, electric-dipole-based topological structures have been observed in dedicated ferroelectric systems, especially ferroelectric–insulator superlattices such as PbTiO<sub>3</sub>/SrTiO<sub>3</sub>, which was later shown to be a model system owing to its high depolarizing field<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR4" title="Yadav, A. K. et al. Observation of polar vortices in oxide superlattices. Nature 530, 198–201 (2016)." target="_blank">4</a>,<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR5" title="Hsu, S.-L. et al. Emergence of the vortex state in confined ferroelectric heterostructures. Adv. Mater. 31, 1901014–1901022 (2019)." target="_blank">5</a>,<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR6" title="Shafer, P. et al. Emergent chirality in the electric polarization texture of titanate superlattices. Proc. Natl Acad. Sci. USA 115, 915–920 (2018)." target="_blank">6</a>,<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR7" title="Gruverman, A. et al. Vortex ferroelectric domains. J. Phys. Condens. Matter 20, 4 (2008)." target="_blank">7</a>,<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR8" title="Nelson, C. T. et al. Spontaneous vortex nanodomain arrays at ferroelectric heterointerfaces. Nano Lett. 11, 828–834 (2011)." target="_blank">8</a></sup>. To date, the electric dipole equivalent of ordered magnetic spin lattices driven by the Dzyaloshinskii–Moriya interaction (DMi)<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR9" title="Dzyaloshinsky, I. Solids 4, 241–255 (1958)." target="_blank">9</a>,<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR10" title="Moriya, T. Anisotropic superexchange interaction and weak ferromagnetism. Phys. Rev. 120, 91–98 (1960)." target="_blank">10</a></sup> has not been experimentally observed. Here we examine a domain structure in a single PbTiO<sub>3</sub> epitaxial layer sandwiched between SrRuO<sub>3</sub> electrodes. We observe periodic clockwise and anticlockwise ferroelectric vortices that are modulated by a second ordering along their toroidal core. The resulting topology, supported by calculations, is a labyrinth-like pattern with two orthogonal periodic modulations that form an incommensurate polar crystal that provides a ferroelectric analogue to the recently discovered incommensurate spin crystals in ferromagnetic materials<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR11" title="Kurumaji, T. et al. Jpn 90, 024705 (2021)." target="_blank">12</a>,<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04260-1#ref-CR13" title="Seddon, S. D. et al. These findings further blur the border between emergent ferromagnetic and ferroelectric topologies, clearing the way for experimental realization of further electric counterparts of magnetic DMi-driven phases.</span></p>                    <br><br>
