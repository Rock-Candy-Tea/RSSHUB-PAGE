The lower single-pass conversion (<10%) of the state-of-the-art CO<sub>2</sub> electrolyzers contributes significantly to the cost of post-CO2RR separation of products, rendering even processes with high CO2RR current densities unfit for scaling up. Here, we develop an aqueous flow-through electrochemical cell to enhance the activity and selectivity of C<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub> on a three-dimensional (3D) Cu mesh electrode by applying square-wave oscillating potentials. A high C<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub> faradaic efficiency of <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "MS Gothic";">～</span>58%, C<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub> current density of 306 mA/cm<sup>2</sup>, and gaseous C<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub> purity of <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "MS Gothic";">～</span>52 wt % without CO<sub>2</sub> in the product stream are obtained. Integrating the 3D Cu mesh catalyst in a photovoltaic (PV) electrolyzer yields a solar-to-carbon (STC) efficiency of <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "MS Gothic";">～</span>10% with a solar-to- C<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub> efficiency of <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "MS Gothic";">～</span>4%.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.xcrp.2022.101053" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Graphical abstract</span></a></p><p style="text-align:center"><br></p><p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://ttps//www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666386422003472" target="_self"><img src="https://cors.zfour.workers.dev/?http://image.sciencenet.cn/home/202209/16/202539km74zjgjeggqed3h.jpg" title alt="fx1.jpg" style="text-align: center; white-space: normal;" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"></a></p>                    <br><br>
