font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">预防老年人癌症的三种简单干预措施</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">（</span><a href="https://www.media.uzh.ch/en/Press-Releases/2022/Cancer-Prevention-in-Older-People.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-family: 宋体;">研究表明，高剂量维生素</span>D(vitamin D)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">、</span>ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸</span>(ω-3 fatty acids)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">simple home strength exercise program</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">简称</span><a href="https://www.media.uzh.ch/en/Press-Releases/2022/Cancer-Prevention-in-Older-People.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">SHEP</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">) <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的组合可以将</span>70<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁以上健康成年人的癌症风险累积降低</span>61%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。这是第一项测试三种负担得起的公共卫生干预措施对预防浸润性癌症的综合益处的研究，结果可能会影响老年人癌症预防的未来。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在欧洲和美国，癌症被认为是一种主要的与年龄有关的疾病。它是老年人死亡的第二大原因，并且患大多数癌症的机会随着年龄的增长而增加。苏黎世大学和苏黎世大学医院（</span>UniversityHospital Zurich<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）老年医学和衰老研究教授海克·比肖夫</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">法拉利（</span>Heike Bischoff-Ferrari<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）表示，除了不吸烟和防晒等预防性建议外，专注于癌症预防的公共卫生工作是有限的：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">中老年人今天的预防工作在很大程度上仅限于筛查和疫苗接种工作。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">维生素</span> D</strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">、</span>ω-3</strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸和运动（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://www.media.uzh.ch/en/Press-Releases/2022/Cancer-Prevention-in-Older-People.html" target="_blank">Vitamin D, ω-3 and exercise</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">先前的研究表明，维生素</span>D<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">可抑制癌细胞的生长。同样，</span>ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸可能会抑制正常细胞转化为癌细胞，而运动已被证明可以改善免疫功能并减少炎症，这可能有助于预防癌症。然而，缺乏强有力的临床研究证明这三种简单干预措施（单独或组合）的有效性。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">海克·比肖夫</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">法拉利和她的同事希望通过单独或组合测试每日高剂量维生素</span>D3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">（一种维生素</span>D<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">补充剂）、每日补充</span>ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸和简单的家庭锻炼计划的效果来填补这些关于</span>70<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁或以上成年人患浸润性癌症风险的知识空白。</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">新型癌症治疗旨在通过结合多种药物来阻断癌症发展的多种途径。我们将这一概念转化为癌症预防，</span>”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">海克·比肖夫</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">法拉利解释说。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简单治疗的组合（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://www.media.uzh.ch/en/Press-Releases/2022/Cancer-Prevention-in-Older-People.html" target="_blank">A combination of simple treatments</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">为此，研究人员进行了</span>DO-HEALTH<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">试验：在</span>5<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">个欧洲国家（瑞士、法国、德国、奥地利和葡萄牙）进行的为期</span>3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年的试验，共有</span>2157<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">名参与者。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">参与者被随机分为</span>8<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">个不同的组，以测试干预措施的个体和综合效益：第一组每天接受</span>2000 IU<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">维生素</span>D3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">（相当于当前老年人推荐量即每天</span> 800 IU<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的</span>250%)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，每天</span>1 g ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸，每周</span>3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">次</span>SHEP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">；第二组接受维生素</span>D3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和</span>ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸；第三组接受维生素</span>D3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和</span>SHEP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">；第</span>4<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">组接受</span>ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸和</span>SHEP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">；第五组只接受维生素</span>D3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">；第六组只接受</span>ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸；第七组只有</span>SHEP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">；最后一组接受安慰剂。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">参与者每</span>3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">个月接到一次检查电话，并在基线、第一年、第二年和第三年在试验中心接受健康和功能的标准化检查。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">预防浸润性癌症（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://www.media.uzh.ch/en/Press-Releases/2022/Cancer-Prevention-in-Older-People.html" target="_blank">Preventing invasive cancer</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">结果表明，所有三种治疗方法（维生素</span>D3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">、</span>ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸和</span>SHEP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）对侵袭性癌症的风险都有累积的益处。每种治疗方法都有很小的个体益处，但当所有三种治疗方法结合使用时，益处变得具有统计学意义，研究人员发现癌症风险总体降低了</span>61%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这是第一个随机对照试验，表明每日维生素</span>D3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">、补充海洋</span>ω-3<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">脂肪酸和简单的家庭锻炼计划的组合可能有效预防</span>70<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁及以上一般健康和活跃的成年人的侵润性癌症，</span> ” <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">海克·比肖夫</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">法拉利评论道。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">结果可能会影响老年人侵润性癌症预防的未来。海克·比肖夫</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">法拉利总结道：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">我们的结果虽然基于多重比较并需要复制，但可能被证明有利于减轻癌症负担。未来的研究应验证联合治疗在预防癌症方面的益处，并延伸到更长时间的后续研究超过本试验评估的三年持续时间。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; color: #3E3D40;">Objective:</span></strong><span style="font-size: 18px; color: #3E3D40;"> The aim of this study was to test the individual and combined benefit of vitamin D, omega-3, and a simple home strength exercise program on the risk of any invasive cancer.</span></span></p><p style="background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"><strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #3E3D40;">Design:</span></strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #3E3D40;"> The DO-HEALTH trial is a three-year, multicenter, 2 × 2 × 2 factorial design double-blind, randomized-controlled trial to test the individual and combined benefit of three public health interventions.</span></span></p><p style="background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"><strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #3E3D40;">Setting:</span></strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #3E3D40;"> The trial was conducted between December 2012 and December 2017 in five European countries.</span></span></p><p style="background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"><strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #3E3D40;">Participants:</span></strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #3E3D40;"> Generally healthy community-dwelling adults ≥70 years were recruited.</span></span></p><p style="background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; color: #3E3D40;">Interventions:</span></strong><span style="font-size: 18px; color: #3E3D40;"> Supplemental 2000 IU/day of vitamin D</span><sub><span style="font-size: 18px; color: #3E3D40;">3</span></sub><span style="font-size: 18px; color: #3E3D40;">, and/or 1 g/day of marine omega-3s, and/or a simple home strength exercise (SHEP) programme compared to placebo and control exercise.</span></span></p><p style="background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Georgia; color: #3E3D40;">Main outcome:</span></strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Georgia; color: #3E3D40;"> In this pre-defined exploratory analysis, time-to-development of any verified invasive cancer was the primary outcome in an adjusted, intent-to-treat analysis.</span></span></p><p style="background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; color: #3E3D40;">Results:</span></strong><span style="font-size: 18px; color: #3E3D40;"> In total, 2,157 participants (mean age 74.9 years; Over a median follow-up of 2.99 years, 81 invasive cancer cases were diagnosed and verified.
