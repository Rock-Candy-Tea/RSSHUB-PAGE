font-family: 宋体;">因子一致，精确到</span>12<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">位以内，这是迄今为止物理学理论和实验最精确的匹配之一。然而，当电子不再是“自由”粒子，即不受其他影响，而是束缚在原子核上时，电子的磁矩就会发生变化。</span>g<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">因子的微小变化可以用</span>QED<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">海德堡马克斯·普朗克核物理研究所的</span>ALPHATRAP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">实验提供了一个专门设计的潘宁陷阱（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-06-quantum-electrodynamics-accurately.html" style="text-decoration: underline; In particular, measurements of the electron’s magnetic moment (or g factor) of highly charged ions in Penning traps provide a stringent probe for QED, which allows testing of the standard model in the strongest electromagnetic fields<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-04807-w#ref-CR6" title="Sturm, S. et al. When studying the differences between isotopes, many common QED contributions cancel owing to the identical electron configuration, making it possible to resolve the intricate effects stemming from the nuclear differences. Experimentally, however, this quickly becomes limited, particularly by the precision of the ion masses or the magnetic field stability<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-04807-w#ref-CR7" title="Köhler, F. et al. Here we report on a measurement technique that overcomes these limitations by co-trapping two highly charged ions and measuring the difference in their g factors directly. We apply a dual Ramsey-type measurement scheme with the ions locked on a common magnetron orbit<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-04807-w#ref-CR8" title="Rainville, S., Thompson, J. & Pritchard, D.
