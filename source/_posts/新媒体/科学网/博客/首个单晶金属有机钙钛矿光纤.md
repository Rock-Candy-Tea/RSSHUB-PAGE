font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Science Advances</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">, 23 Sep 2022, Vol 8, Issue 38. </span><a href="https://dx.doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.abq8629" target="_blank" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.abq8629</span></a> </p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">参与此项研究的除了伦敦玛丽皇后大学的研究人员之外，还有来自英国伦敦大学学院（</span>University College London<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的研究人员。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">光纤是一种像头发一样细的细线，其中光以超高速传播，比电缆中的电子快</span>100<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">倍。这些微小的光纤传输着我们互联网上的大部分数据。目前，大多数光纤是由玻璃制成的。苏雷博士团队制造的钙钛矿型光纤（</span>perovskite </span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/optical+fiber/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">optical fiber</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）仅由一块钙钛矿晶体组成。光纤的纤芯宽度低至</span>50 μm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">（相当于人头发丝的大小），并且非常灵活。与多晶体光纤相比，光纤可以弯曲到</span>3.5 mm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的半径。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">与多晶有机金属钙钛矿相比，单晶有机金属钙钛矿更稳定、更高效、更耐用、缺陷更少。因此，科学家们一直在寻求制造单晶钙钛矿型光纤，从而为光纤带来这种高效率（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/high+efficiency/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">high efficiency</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">伦敦玛丽皇后大学的光子学高级讲师苏雷博士表示：“单晶钙钛矿光纤可以集成到当前的光纤网络中，以替代该系统中的关键组件，例如更高效的激光和能量转换，提高我们宽带网络的速度和质量。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">苏雷博士的团队通过使用一种新的温度生长方法，能够在液体溶液（运行成本非常低廉）中生长并精确控制单晶有机金属钙钛矿纤维的长度和直径。在此过程中，他们逐渐改变加热位置、线接触和温度，以确保长度的连续增长，同时防止宽度的随机增长。通过这种方法，可以控制纤维的长度，并且可以改变钙钛矿纤维芯（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/perovskite/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">perovskite</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"> fiber core<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的横截面。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">根据他们的预测，由于单晶质量，他们的光纤在几个月内具有良好的稳定性，传输损耗小于</span>0.7 dB/cm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，足以制作光学器件。它们具有很大的柔韧性（可以弯曲到</span>3.5 mm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的半径），并且比多晶对应物（具有类似长度的多晶</span>MAPbBr<sub>3</sub><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">毫线光电探测器）具有更大的光电流值。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">苏雷博士说：“这项技术也可以作为高分辨率探测器用于医学成像（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/medical+imaging/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">medical imaging</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）。与最新技术相比，纤维的小直径可以用来捕获更小的像素。因此，这意味着通过使用我们的纤维，我们可以获得微米级的像素，为医生提供更高的分辨率图像，以便更好、更准确地进行诊断。我们还可以将这些纤维用于吸收光线的纺织品中。然后，当我们穿着例如衣服或将这种纤维编织到纺织品中的设备时，它们可以将太阳能（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/solar+energy/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">solar energy</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）转换为电能。因此，我们可以拥有太阳能衣服。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">本研究得到了英国工程和物理科学研究委员会（</span><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.13039/501100000266" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Semiconductors in their optical-fiber forms are desirable. Single-crystal organometallic halide perovskites have attractive optoelectronic properties and therefore are suitable fiber-optic platforms. However, single-crystal organometallic perovskite optical fibers have not been reported before due to the challenge of one-directional single-crystal growth in solution. Here, we report a solution-processed approach to continuously grow single-crystal organometallic perovskite optical fibers with controllable diameters and lengths. For single-crystal MAPbBr<sub>3</sub> (MA = CH<sub>3</sub>NH<sub>3</sub><sup>+</sup>) perovskite optical fiber made using our method, it demonstrates low transmission losses (<0.7 dB/cm), mechanical flexibilities (a bending radius down to 3.5 mm), and mechanical deformation–tunable photoluminescence in organometallic perovskites. Moreover, the light confinement provided by our organometallic perovskite optical fibers leads to three-photon absorption (3PA), in contrast with 2PA in bulk single crystals under the same experimental conditions. The single-crystal organometallic perovskite optical fibers have the potential in future optoelectronic applications.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
