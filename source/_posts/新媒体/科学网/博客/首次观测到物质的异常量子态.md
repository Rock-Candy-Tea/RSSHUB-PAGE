font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">安德里亚•比安奇解释说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“当加入第三个电子时，电子的自旋不能对齐，因为相邻的两个电子的自旋总是相反的，这就产生了我们所说的磁阻挫（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-05-unusual-quantum-state.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">那么它们是如何添加第三个电子并引起如此大的受挫的呢</span>?</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span>Ce<sub>2</sub>Zr<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。他在开发超导体等先进材料（</span> </span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/advanced+materials/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-family: 宋体;">从梦想到现实（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-05-unusual-quantum-state.html" target="_blank">From dream to reality</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">a frustrated magnet</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The Ce<sup>3+</sup> pseudospin-1/2 degrees of freedom in the pyrochlore magnet Ce<sub>2</sub>Zr<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> are known to possess dipole-octupole character, making it a candidate for novel quantum spin liquid ground states at low temperatures. We report new polarized neutron diffraction at low temperatures, as well as heat capacity (<em>C<sub>p</sub></em>) measurements on single crystal Ce<sub>2</sub>Zr<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub>. The former bears both similarities and differences with that measured from the canonical dipolar spin ice compound Ho<sub>2</sub>Ti<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub>, while the latter rises sharply at low temperatures, initially plateauing near 0.08 K, before falling off toward a high temperature zero beyond 3 K. Above<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "MS Gothic";">～</span>0.5  K, the <em>C<sub>p</sub></em> dataset can be fit to the results of a quantum numerical linked cluster calculation, carried out to fourth order, that allows estimates for the terms in the near-neighbor XYZ Hamiltonian expected for such dipole-octupole pyrochlore systems. Fits of the same theory to the temperature dependence of the magnetic susceptibility and unpolarized neutron scattering complement this analysis. A comparison between the resulting best-fit numerical linked cluster calculation and the polarized neutron diffraction shows both agreement and discrepancies, mostly in the form of zone-boundary diffuse scattering in the non-spin-flip channel, which are attributed to interactions beyond near neighbors. The lack of an observed thermodynamic anomaly and the constraints on the near-neighbor XYZ Hamiltonian suggest that Ce<sub>2</sub>Zr<sub>2</sub>O<sub>7</sub> realizes a U(1)<sub>π</sub> quantum spin liquid state at low temperatures, and one that likely resides near the boundary between dipolar and octupolar character.</span></p>                    <br><br>
