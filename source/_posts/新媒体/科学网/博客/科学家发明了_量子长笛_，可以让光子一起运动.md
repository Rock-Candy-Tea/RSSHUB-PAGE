font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">quantum bits</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）中一样，你可以在整个乐器中发送一个或几个波长的光子，每个波长创建一个‘音符’（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-07-scientists-quantum-flute-particles.html" target="_self">'note'</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">但他们最奇怪的发现是光子的行为方式。在自然界中，光子几乎从不相互作用——它们只是相互穿过。经过艰苦的准备，科学家有时可以促使两个光子对彼此的存在做出反应。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">大卫·舒斯特说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“在这里，我们做了一些更奇怪的事情。一开始，光子完全不相互作用，但当系统中的总能量（</span> </span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/total+energy/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">total energy</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）达到临界点时，突然之间，它们都在相互交谈。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">在实验室实验中，有这么多光子相互“交谈”是极其奇怪的，就像看到一只猫用后腿走路一样。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">大卫·舒斯特说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“通常情况下，大多数粒子的相互作用是两个粒子一对一地相互弹跳或吸引。如果你添加第三个粒子，它们通常仍然会与其中一个粒子进行顺序互动。但这个系统让它们同时相互作用。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">他们的实验一次只测试了</span>5<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">个“音符”，但科学家们最终可以想象通过一个量子比特来控制数百或数千个“音符”。对于像量子计算机这样复杂的操作，工程师们想要在任何地方尽可能地简化，大卫·舒斯特说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“如果你想建造一个有</span>1000<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">比特的量子计算机，并且你可以通过一个比特控制所有的量子计算机，这将是非常有价值的。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">研究人员也对这种行为本身感到兴奋。没有人在自然界中观察到任何类似的相互作用，所以研究人员也希望这一发现可以用于模拟地球上看不到的复杂物理现象，甚至可能包括一些黑洞的物理现象。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">除此之外，这些实验很有趣。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“通常情况下，量子相互作用发生的长度和时间尺度太小或太快，我们无法看到。在我们的系统中，我们可以在任何音符中测量单个光子（</span>single </span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/photons/" style="text-decoration: underline; We demonstrate a variety of protocols for universal single-mode quantum control applicable across all cavity modes, using only a single drive line. Recently, a number of artificial quantum systems have emerged as simulators for many-body physics, featuring the ability to engineer strong interactions. However, the interactions in these systems have largely been limited to the two-body paradigm and require building up multibody interactions by combining two-body forces. Here we implement a scheme to create a higher-order interaction between photons stored in multiple electromagnetic modes of a microwave cavity. The system is dressed such that there is collectively no interaction until a target total photon number is reached across multiple distinct modes, at which point the photons interact strongly. In our demonstration, we create interactions involving up to three bodies and across up to five modes.
