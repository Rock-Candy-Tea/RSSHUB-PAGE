font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">HOM</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">) <span style="font-size: 18px; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">bunching</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span>”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。当在分裂光束的路径末端使用光电探测器测量纠缠光子时，光的输出概率图中的特征</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">下降（</span>dip<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span>”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">表明成束光子仅到达一个探测器，而不是另一个探测器。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这个倾角就是洪</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">欧</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">曼德尔效应（</span></span><a href="https://www.myscience.org/news/2022/imaging_breakthrough_could_aid_development_of_quantum_microscopes-2022-glasgow" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Hong-Ou-Mandel effect</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">），它展示了两个光子的完美纠缠。它已被用于量子计算机中的逻辑门等应用中，这些应用需要完美的纠缠才能工作。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">它还被用于量子传感，方法是在分束器的一个输出和光电探测器之间放置一个透明表面，从而在探测光子所需的时间中引入非常轻微的延迟。对延迟的复杂分析可以帮助重建表面厚度等细节。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">现在，格拉斯哥大学领导的团队已将其应用于显微镜，使用单光子敏感相机来测量聚束和反聚束光子（</span><a href="https://www.myscience.org/news/2022/imaging_breakthrough_could_aid_development_of_quantum_microscopes-2022-glasgow" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">bunched and anti-bunched photons</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）并解析表面的显微图像。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在《自然光子学》发表的论文中，他们展示了他们如何使用他们的设置来创建高分辨率图像，这些图像是一些透明的丙烯酸喷涂在平均深度为</span> 13 μm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的显微镜载玻片上，并在一块玻璃上蚀刻了一组拼写为</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">格拉斯哥大学</span>(University of Glasgow)”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的字母。约</span> 8 μm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">深。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">他们的结果表明，可以创建分辨率在</span> 1 ~ 10 μm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）教授是该论文的通讯作者。达妮埃莱·法乔教授说：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">使用可见光的传统显微镜技术教会了我们大量关于自然世界的知识，并帮助我们取得了一系列令人难以置信的技术进步。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">然而，它确实有一些限制，可以通过使用量子光探测微观领域来克服。在生物成像中，细胞几乎可以完全透明，能够在不使用传统光的情况下检查它们的精细细节可能是一个主要优势</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">我们选择在这项研究中精确地对透明表面进行成像以展示这种潜力。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">同样，传统显微镜中的样品需要保持完全静止</span>——<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">即使是很小的振动也会引入一定程度的模糊，从而破坏图像。然而，</span>HOM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">干涉只需要测量光子相关性，对稳定性的需求要少得多。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">现在我们已经确定可以通过利用洪</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">欧</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">曼德尔效应来构建这种量子显微镜，我们热衷于改进这项技术，使其能够解析纳米级图像。这需要一些聪明的工程实现这一目标，但能够清楚地看到细胞膜甚至</span> DNA <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">链等极小特征的前景令人兴奋。我们期待继续改进我们的设计。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41566-022-00980-6" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">原文</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">或者</span><a href="https://www.myscience.org/news/2022/imaging_breakthrough_could_aid_development_of_quantum_microscopes-2022-glasgow" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">相关报道</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41566-022-00980-6" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Hong–Ou–Mandel (HOM) interference—the bunching of indistinguishable photons at a beamsplitter—is a staple of quantum optics and lies at the heart of many quantum sensing approaches and recent optical quantum computers. Here we report a full-field, scan-free quantum imaging technique that exploits HOM interference to reconstruct the surface depth profile of transparent samples. We demonstrate the ability to retrieve images with micrometre-scale depth features with photon flux as small as seven photon pairs per frame. Using a single-photon avalanche diode camera, we measure both bunched and anti-bunched photon-pair distributions at the output of an HOM interferometer, which are combined to provide a lower-noise image of the sample. This approach demonstrates the possibility of HOM microscopy as a tool for the label-free imaging of transparent samples in the very low photon regime.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
