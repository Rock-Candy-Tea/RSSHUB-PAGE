font-family: 宋体;">）是此研究论文的通讯作者。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">此研究表明，在老年早期（</span>70-75<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁）每天进行</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">分钟的中度到剧烈运动可能最好地避免老年晚期（</span>80<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁以上）的主要心脏病，包括心力衰竭。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">相关的编辑总结道，在锻炼方面，这些发现强化了</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">迟做总比不做好</span>”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的格言，但在老年时，越早越好。众所周知，无论性别和种族，体育锻炼都与降低心血管疾病的风险和延长寿命有关，而且好处随着付出的努力而增加。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">但是，只有相对较少的研究专门研究了晚年运动是否有助于预防老年心脏病和中风。为了填补这一知识空白，研究人员利用了</span>Progetto Veneto Anziani (ProVA)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的数据，该研究对</span>3099<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">名意大利老年人（≥</span>65<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁）进行了研究。</span> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在</span>1995-1997<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年间进行了初步评估，包括详细的病史、体格检查、扫描和一系列血液测试，并在</span>4<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年和</span>7<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年后进行了两次进一步的评估。</span> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在研究开始时，女性比男性更有可能同时患有</span>4<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">种以上的疾病，骨关节炎、骨质疏松症和慢性肾病的患病率更高；慢性阻塞性肺病（</span>chronic obstructive pulmonary disease<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>COPD<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）和糖尿病在男性中更为常见。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">参与者在每个时间点填写了有关其体育活动水平的问卷。适度的体育活动包括散步、滚球和钓鱼，而剧烈的体育活动包括园艺、健身房锻炼、骑自行车、跳舞和游泳。</span> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">那些每天体育活动加起来达到</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">分钟或以上的人被定义为活跃；那些低于这个时间的人被定义为不活跃。男性比女性更有可能进行体育锻炼。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">体育活动模式的变化被定义为：稳定</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">低（不活动</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">不活动）；高下降（活动</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">不活动）；低增加（不活动</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">活动）；和稳定高（活动</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">活动）。</span> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">还收集了有关家庭收入、教育程度、家庭成员人数以及吸烟和饮酒的其他潜在关键背景信息。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">然后通过链接到截至</span>2018<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年</span>12<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">月底的出院记录和死亡证明来跟踪所有参与者的健康状况。最终分析包括</span>2754<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">名数据完整的参与者，其中</span>1398<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">人为女性（占</span>60%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">监测期内，新增心脏病、心力衰竭、脑卒中确诊</span>1037<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">例。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">随着时间的推移，增加体育活动水平以及保持积极的生活方式与男性和女性心血管疾病和死亡风险的降低有关。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">观察到晚年新发冠心病和心力衰竭病例的风险降低幅度最大。没有观察到体育活动与中风之间的显著关联。</span> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">随着时间的推移，大多数参与者都有稳定的积极体育活动模式。与稳定低强度运动模式相比，稳定高强度运动模式与男性心血管疾病风险显著降低（</span>52%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）相关。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">最大的好处似乎发生在</span>70<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁。风险在</span>75<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁时仅略低，在</span>80-85<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁时没有降低，这表明在老年早期改善身体活动可能会产生最大的影响，例如研究人员。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">还观察到运动长度的</span>J<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">形曲线，每天进行</span>20~40<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">分钟的中度到剧烈的体育活动，心脏病和心力衰竭的减少最为明显。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">虽然观察到的关联在男性中最强，但研究人员强调：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">尽管风险降低没有达到统计学意义，但进行更多体育活动的女性几乎所有心血管结局的发生率始终较低，但在考虑总体死亡率时，风险是显著减少。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">这是一项观察性研究，因此无法确定原因。研究人员承认，该研究依赖于参与者的回忆，对体育活动水平进行了主观评估，并且没有关于中年体育活动水平的数据，所有这些都可能影响晚年的心血管风险状况。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">尽管如此，他们得出的结论是：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这些结果表明，鉴于在降低心血管风险方面可能更有效，公共卫生政策的目标应该是促进或开始在中年和早期的晚年进行体育锻炼。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">应建议每天至少进行</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">分钟的中度至剧烈运动，以达到最大的心血管益处。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在一篇相关的编辑评论中，意大利的里雅斯特大学（</span>University of Trieste, Italy<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的</span> <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">恩里科·法布里斯（</span>Enrico Fabris<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）和詹弗兰科·辛娜拉（</span>Gianfranco Sinagra<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）博士解释说，体育活动有助于改善动脉血流，并可能减少其粘性和血栓的形成。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">然而，</span>[<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">体育活动</span>]<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">可以降低</span>[<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">心血管疾病</span>]<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">未来风险的详细机制仍未完全了解，</span>”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">他们指出。</span>“[<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">体育活动</span>]<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的有利效果可以简单地解释为它能够通过更好地控制血压、血糖水平和血脂来减缓动脉粥样硬化过程。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">但研究结果表明：</span>“‘<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">运动就是良药</span>’<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">同样适用于晚年。即使是少量的</span> [<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">体育锻炼</span>] <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">也可能对老年人产生有益的影响，但如果尽早动起来而不是晚动会更好，</span>”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">他们总结道。</span> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览原文或者相关报道。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://heart.bmj.com/content/heartjnl/108/5/360.full.pdf" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="margin: 0px 0px 5px; background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"><strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #333333;">Introduction</span></strong><span style="font-family: "Times New Roman"; color: #333333;"> Cardiovascular benefits deriving from physical activity are well known, but it is unclear whether physical activity trajectories in late life are associated with different risks of cardiovascular diseases.</span></span></p><p style="margin: 0px 0px 5px; background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"><strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #333333;">Methods</span></strong><span style="font-family: "Times New Roman"; color: #333333;"> Progetto Veneto Anziani (Pro.V.A.) is a cohort study of 3099 Italians aged ≥65 years with baseline assessment in 1995–1997 and follow-up visits at 4 and 7 years. Surveillance was extended to 2018 by linkage with hospital and mortality records. Prevalent and incident cardiovascular diseases (coronary heart disease, heart failure and stroke) were identified through clinical examination, questionnaire, or hospital records. Moderate to vigorous physical activity was considered as a time-varying variable. Physical activity trajectories were categorised as: stable-low, high-decreasing, low-increasing and stable-high. Exposure was also assessed at 70, 75, 80 and 85 years.</span></span></p><p style="margin: 0px 0px 5px; background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"><strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #333333;">Results</span></strong><span style="font-family: "Times New Roman"; color: #333333;"> Overall, physical activity was associated with lower rates of incident cardiovascular diseases. A significant risk reduction was present among men and was stronger earlier in late life (70–75 years). Trajectories of stable-high physical activity were associated with a significantly lower risk of cardiovascular outcomes among men (HR 0.48, 95% CI 0.27 to 0.86) compared with those with stable-low trajectories (p for trend 0.002). No significant association was found with stroke. The greatest cardiovascular risk reduction was observed for >20 min/day of physical activity, and was more marked at 70 years.</span></span></p><p style="margin: 0px 0px 5px; background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"><strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; color: #333333;">Conclusion</span></strong><span style="font-family: "Times New Roman"; color: #333333;"> Increasingly active trajectories of physical activity were associated with lower rates of cardiovascular diseases and overall mortality. Promoting at least 20 min/day of physical activity early in late life seems to provide the greatest cardiovascular benefits.</span></span></p><p style="margin: 0px 0px 5px; background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"><span style="font-family: "Times New Roman"; color: #333333;">微信中也有类似的报道：</span></span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">世界卫生组织认定，走路是“世界上最好的运动”。数据统计：每走一步，可推动人体</span><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-family: "times new roman";">5<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）增强心脏功能</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">不爱运动，所以心脏不好，步行能增强心脏功能，使心脏慢而有力。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">6<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）打通血管</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">医学讲，步行能增强血管弹性，减少血管破裂的可能性，也能够增强全身弹性。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">9<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）减少五脏疾病</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">五脏六腑需要运动，如果不运动，自然就硬化而得病，因此，步行可以增强消化腺的分泌功能，促进胃肠有规律的蠕动，增加食欲，增强五脏六腑弹性的功能，很多疾病自然就治愈。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">11<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）精神快乐</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">藏医学讲，定时坚持步行，会消除心脏缺血性症状或降低血压。使人体消除疲劳，精神愉快，缓解心慌心悸。人不愉快与血性有关系，步行能降低血糖，血脂；排除血淤，血症等血里的垃圾，血里没有垃圾，气血通畅，人会愉快。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px;
