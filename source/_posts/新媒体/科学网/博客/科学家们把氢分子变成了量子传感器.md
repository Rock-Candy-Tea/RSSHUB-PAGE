font-family: "times new roman";">STM<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">尖端和样品之间的空间几乎是难以想象的小，大约</span>6 Å<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">或</span>0.6 nm<span style="font-size: 18px; line-height: 2em;"><span style="color: #262626; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The coupling of coherent quantum systems to the surrounding environment can be used to create highly sensitive sensors, but many surface-based implementations, such as nitrogen vacancies in diamond, have limited spatial resolution. Wang <em>et al</em>. created a quantum sensor with high spatial sensitivity by using the electric field from a scanning tunneling microscope tip to confine a hydrogen molecule on copper nitride islands grown on a copper surface. Femtosecond-pulse terahertz spectroscopy was used to follow the coherence of the two-level system created by different adsorption geometries. The temporal oscillations and decoherence in the superposition state showed spatial variations at the sub-angstrom scale. —PDS</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abn9220" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="background: white; line-height: 2em;"><span style="color: #262626; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">A scanning tunneling microscope (STM) combined with a pump-probe femtosecond terahertz (THz) laser can enable coherence measurements of single molecules. We report THz pump-probe measurements that demonstrate quantum sensing based on a hydrogen (H<sub>2</sub>) molecule in the cavity created with an STM tip near a surface. Atomic-scale spatial and femtosecond temporal resolutions were obtained from this quantum coherence. The H<sub>2</sub> acts as a two-level system, with its coherent superposition exhibiting extreme sensitivity to the applied electric field and the underlying atomic composition of the copper nitride (Cu<sub>2</sub>N) monolayer islands grown on a Cu(100) surface. We acquired time-resolved images of THz rectification of H<sub>2</sub> over Cu<sub>2</sub>N islands for variable pump-probe delay times to visualize the heterogeneity of the chemical environment at sub-angstrom scale.</span></p>                    <br><br>
