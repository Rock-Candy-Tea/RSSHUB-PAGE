<p style="line-height: 3em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 24px;">带状疱疹病毒感染会增加人患痴呆症的风险吗？</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">诸平</span></p><p style="text-align: center; height: 376px;" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"></a><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">据《科技日报》（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/do-shingles-viral-infections-increase-a-persons-risk-of-dementia/" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">scitechdaily</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-family: 宋体;">）网站</span> 2022<span style="font-family: 宋体;">年</span>7<span style="font-family: 宋体;">月</span>18<span style="font-family: 宋体;">日报道，</span></span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/tag/american-academy-of-neurology/" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">美国神经病学学会</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/tag/american-academy-of-neurology/" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">American Academy Of Neurology</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>AAN<span style="font-family: 宋体;">）对于“带状疱疹病毒感染（</span></span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/do-shingles-viral-infections-increase-a-persons-risk-of-dementia/" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">Shingles Viral Infections</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）会增加人患痴呆症的风险吗（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/do-shingles-viral-infections-increase-a-persons-risk-of-dementia/" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">Do Shingles Viral Infections Increase a Person’s Risk of Dementia?</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）”这一疑问给出了确切地回答，答案是否定的。</span><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">带状疱疹是一种由水痘病毒（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/do-shingles-viral-infections-increase-a-persons-risk-of-dementia/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">chickenpox virus</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）重新激活引起的病毒感染，会导致身体一侧或面部因神经炎症而出现疼痛的水疱皮疹。由于这种炎症，有科学推测带状疱疹可能会增加人患痴呆症的风险。然而，根据新研究的结果，带状疱疹与痴呆风险的增加无关。该研究于</span>2022<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年</span>6<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">月</span>8<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">日已经在美国神经病学学会</span>(</span><a href="https://www.aan.com/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">AAN</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的医学杂志《神经病学》（</span></span><a href="https://n.neurology.org/content/early/2022/06/08/WNL.0000000000200709" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Neurology</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）杂志网站发表——</span>Sigrun Alba Johannesdottir Schmidt, Katalin Veres, Henrik Toft Sørensen, Niels Obel, Victor W. Henderson. Incident Herpes Zoster and Risk of Dementia: A Population-Based Danish Cohort Study. Neurology, First Published: 8 June 2022, </span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1212/WNL.0000000000200709%C2%A0" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">DOI: 10.1212/WNL.0000000000200709</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">. </span><a href="https://n.neurology.org/content/early/2022/06/08/WNL.0000000000200709" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">https://n.neurology.org/content/early/2022/06/08/WNL.0000000000200709</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">参与此项研究的是丹麦（</span>Denmark<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）和美国（</span>USA<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的研究人员。他们包括来自丹麦奥胡斯大学医院（</span>Aarhus University Hospital, Denmark<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）、丹麦哥本哈根大学医院（</span>Copenhagen University Hospital, Rigshospitalet, Denmark<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），以及美国加州斯坦福大学（</span>Stanford University, California, USA<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的研究人员。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">科学家推测，带状疱疹可能会增加患痴呆症（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/do-shingles-viral-infections-increase-a-persons-risk-of-dementia/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">dementia</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）的风险。然而，新的研究发现，带状疱疹与痴呆风险的增加无关。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">丹麦奥胡斯大学医院（</span>Aarhus University Hospital in Denmark<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的医学博士希格露恩·阿尔芭·约翰内斯道蒂·施密特（</span>Sigrun Alba Johannesdottir Schmidt<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）说：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">随着一个人年龄的增长，他们患痴呆症的风险也在增加，确定哪些因素可能导致这种风险非常重要。带状疱疹最常影响</span>50<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁以上的人。好消息是，我们的研究发现它似乎不会增加一个人患痴呆症的风险。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">为了此研究科学家们审查了丹麦医学登记处的相关资料。他们确定了</span>247305<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">名曾因带状疱疹到医院就诊或接受过带状疱疹抗病毒药物治疗的人，以及</span>1235890<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">名年龄和性别相匹配且在</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年内没有患病的人，平均年龄为</span>64<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">然后，研究人员检查了哪些参与者在带状疱疹诊断后长达</span>21<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年发展为痴呆症。只有</span>9.7%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的带状疱疹患者最终患上了痴呆症，但是，没有带状疱疹的人群中却有</span>10.3%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的人患上了痴呆症。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在调整了糖尿病、癌症和颅脑外伤等其他健康状况后，科学家们发现，带状疱疹患者患痴呆症的风险比没有患带状疱疹的人反而低</span>7%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">希格露恩·阿尔芭·约翰内斯道蒂·施密特说：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">我们对这些结果感到惊讶。这种风险降低的原因尚不清楚，但可以通过未确诊痴呆症患者漏诊带状疱疹来解释。鼓励老年人接种带状疱疹疫苗，因为它可以预防疾病的并发症，但我们的研究表明，带状疱疹它不太可能降低痴呆症的风险。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">研究人员确实发现，带状疱疹已扩散到中枢神经系统的人患痴呆症的风险几乎增加了一倍。然而，希格露恩·阿尔芭·约翰内斯道蒂·施密特说，这种并发症很少见，影响不到</span> 0.1% <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的带状疱疹患者。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">该研究存在的一个局限性就在于，参与者是根据抗病毒处方或对该疾病的医院就诊来确定的，因此对于病情较轻的人和未接受该疾病治疗的人来说，结果可能不同。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">该研究得到了埃德尔和威廉·多本默克斯慈善基金会（</span><a href="https://n.neurology.org/content/early/2022/06/08/WNL.0000000000200709" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Edel and Wilhelm Daubenmerkls Charitable Foundation</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）的支持。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="https://n.neurology.org/content/early/2022/06/08/WNL.0000000000200709" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">原文</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">或者</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/do-shingles-viral-infections-increase-a-persons-risk-of-dementia/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">相关报道</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://n.neurology.org/content/early/2022/06/08/WNL.0000000000200709" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong>Background and Objectives</strong>: Herpes zoster is caused by reactivation of the neurotrophic varicella-zoster virus. Zoster may contribute to development of dementia through neuroinflammation, cerebral vasculopathy, or direct neural damage, but epidemiological evidence is limited. We used data from linked nationwide Danish registries to conduct a cohort study of the association between zoster and dementia during 1997 to 2017. As secondary aims, we examined if associations were more pronounced for zoster involving cranial nerves (mainly ophthalmic zoster) or the central nervous system and Alzheimer’s disease as an outcome.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong>Methods</strong>: We included people aged ≥40 years with zoster and a general population comparison cohort matched 5︰1 by sex and birth year. We identified zoster and dementia in the registries using prescription records in the community and hospital diagnoses. We used Cox regression to compute confounder-adjusted hazard ratios (HR) with 95% confidence intervals (CIs) for dementia associated with zoster during 0–1 year and 1–21 years of follow-up. We compared the cumulative incidence of dementia, inverse probability-weighted for confounders.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong>Results</strong>: The study included 247,305 people with zoster and 1,235,890 matched general population comparators (median age 64 years; 61% female). The HR of all-cause dementia was 0.98 (95% CI: 0.92–1.04) during the first year and 0.93 (95% CI: 0.90–0.95) thereafter in people with zoster versus matched comparators. Dementia was diagnosed in 9.7% of zoster patients and 10.3% of matched comparators by end of follow-up. We observed no increased long-term risk of dementia in subgroup analyses, except possibly in people with central nervous system infection (HR 1.94; 95% CI: 0.78–4.80). Analyses of Alzheimer’s disease as a separate outcome showed similar results.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong>Discussion</strong>: Herpes zoster is not associated with increased risk of dementia, and contrary to expectation we found a small decrease in risk. The explanation for this finding is unclear, and systematic errors should be considered. Patients with central nervous system involvement had almost two-fold increased relative risk of dementia. The population attributable fraction of dementia due to this rare complication is estimated at 0.014%. Therefore, universal vaccination against varicella-zoster virus in the elderly is unlikely to reduce dementia risk.</span></p>                    <br><br>
