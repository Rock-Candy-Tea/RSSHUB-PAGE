font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">当前的全球气候危机和我们迅速减少的能源资源促使人们寻找更清洁的替代燃料，如氢燃料。与化石燃料不同，氢气在氧气中燃烧时，会产生大量的能量，但不会产生有害的温室气体。不幸的是，当今生产的大多数氢燃料来自天然气或化石燃料，这最终还是增加了其碳足迹（</span>carbon footprint<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">氨</span>(NH<sub>3</sub>)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">是一种碳中性的氢化合物（</span>carbon-neutral </span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/hydrogen/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">hydrogen</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"> compound<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），因其具有较高的能量密度和贮氢能力而备受关注。它可以被分解释放出氮气和氢气。氨可以很容易地液化、储存、运输，并在需要时转化为氢燃料（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/hydrogen+fuel/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">hydrogen fuel</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）。然而，从氨生产氢是一个缓慢的反应，需要很高的能量。为了加快生产速度，通常使用金属催化剂，这也有助于降低氢生产过程中的总能耗。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">近年来研究发现，镍是一种很有前途的氨裂解催化剂。氨被吸附在镍催化剂的表面，随后氨中的氮和氢之间的键被打破，它们作为单独的气体被释放出来。然而，使用镍催化剂获得良好的氨转化通常涉及非常高的操作温度。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在《美国化学会催化》（</span><em>ACS Catalysis</em><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）杂志网站最近发表的一项研究中，日本东京工业大学的一个研究小组，由副教授北野政明</span>(</span><a href="https://www.mces.titech.ac.jp/authors/kitano/profile.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Masaaki Kitano</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">领导，描述了一个解决镍基催化剂面临的问题的解决方案。他们开发了一种最先进的钙酰亚胺</span>(calcium imide, CaNH)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">负载镍催化剂，可以在较低的操作温度下实现良好的氨转化。北野政明博士解释说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“我们的目标是开发一种高效节能的高活性催化剂。在催化剂体系中加入金属酰亚胺不仅提高了催化活性，而且帮助我们揭示了这种体系难以捉摸的工作机制。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">研究小组发现，</span>CaNH<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的存在导致催化剂表面形成</span>NH<sup>2-</sup><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">空位</span>(NH<sup>2-</sup> vacancies <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>V<sub>NH</sub>)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。这些活性物质使得</span>Ni/CaNH<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在比</span>Ni<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">基催化剂低</span>100 <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">℃的反应温度下的催化性能得到了提高。研究人员还开发了计算模型，并进行了同位素标记，以了解催化剂表面发生了什么。计算提出了一种</span>Mars van Krevelen<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">机理（</span>Mars−van Krevelen mechanism<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），包括氨在</span>CaNH<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">表面的吸附，氨在</span>NH<sup>2-</sup><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">空位位置的活化，氮和氢的形成，以及镍纳米颗粒促进空位位置的再生。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">该（</span>Ni/CaNH<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）催化剂具有很高的活性和耐用性，可用于氨制氢气。同时，本研究提供的对催化机理的深入了解，也可用于开发新一代催化剂。北野政明博士总结道</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“全世界都在共同努力建设一个可持续的未来，我们的研究旨在解决我们在实现更清洁的氢燃料经济的道路上所面临的问题。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">这是世界低碳使命（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-08-ammonia-catalyst-hydrogen-temperatures.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">world's low carbon-emission mission</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）的一线希望。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.1c01934" style="text-decoration: underline; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></strong></a></p><p><img src="https://cors.zfour.workers.dev/?http://image.sciencenet.cn/home/202108/31/204644ynhfdunw327afway.jpg" title alt="Snap3.jpg" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Nickel has been studied as an alternative catalyst to ruthenium for ammonia decomposition. However, high reaction temperatures are generally required to achieve good ammonia conversion because the weak interactions between nitrogen and the Ni surface reduce the frequency of the dehydrogenation reaction of ammonia. Here, we report Ni-supported CaNH as a highly efficient catalyst for ammonia decomposition through an NH<sup>2–</sup> vacancy-mediated Mars–van Krevelen mechanism. Ni/CaNH exhibited much greater ammonia decomposition activity than state-of-the-art Ni-based catalysts, and the operating temperature was 100 <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">℃</span> lower than those of the other Ni-based catalysts. Isotope-labeling experiments, NH<sub>3</sub>-pulse measurements, and density functional theory calculations suggested that two anionic electrons at the NH<sup>2–</sup> vacancy on the Ni/CaNH surface activated ammonia molecules to form intermediate NH<em><sub>x</sub></em> (<em>x</em> = 1, 2) species, even at 50 <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">℃</span>, and the supported Ni metal promoted the regeneration of NH<sup>2–</sup> vacancies on CaNH. These results demonstrate that NH<sup>2–</sup> vacancy sites on CaNH play an important role in overcoming the drawbacks of Ni-based catalysts in ammonia decomposition.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
