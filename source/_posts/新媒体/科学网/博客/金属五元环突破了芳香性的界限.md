font-family: 宋体;">芳香性（</span>Aromaticity<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）是化学中最基本的概念之一，可以追溯到德国化学家奥古斯特·凯库勒（</span>August Kekul<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">é）。自</span>1865<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年他的开创性工作以来，研究人员一直试图找到一个通用的定义，并继续发现新类型的芳香性。对于一个被认为是芳香族的分子，它必须在环上分布至少两个电子。然而，在大多数情况下，有更多的电子，从</span>6<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">个到目前为止最多</span>162<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">个电子不等。虽然最大电子数的记录一直在不断提高，但之前的假设是，所需的最少两个电子只能稳定最多四个原子的环。这是因为环越大，每个原子可用的额外电子就越少，这就是为什么稳定效应（</span>stabilizing effect<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）变得越来越小的原因。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">令人惊讶的结论：两个电子可以稳定含有五个原子的环（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-06-metallic-five-membered-boundaries-aromaticity.html" target="_blank">Surprising conclusion: two electrons can stabilize rings with five atoms</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">最近接任了开姆尼茨工业大学无机化学教授职位的罗伯特·克雷奇默博士领导的团队，现在已经成功地改变了这一界限。他们合成了一种含有</span>5<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">个镓原子（</span>gallium atoms<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，</span>Ga<sub>5</sub><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的平面环状的化合物。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“当我们看到其晶体结构（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/crystal+structure/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">crystal structure</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）时，我们印象深刻，”罗伯特·克雷奇默说，“因为环是平的，环内的键具有相似的间距，这一事实已经清楚地表明了分子的芳香特性。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">光谱研究进一步证明了其芳香性质。与柏林洪堡大学的奥利弗·杜梅勒（</span>Oliver Dumele<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）和马尔堡</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">菲利普大学的弗洛里安·魏根德（</span>Florian Weigend<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）研究小组合作，也对这种新分子进行了计算研究。结果：“该化合物是芳香族的，尽管只是很弱，但如果</span>2<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">个电子必须分布在五环原子上，这是可以预料到的，”罗伯特·克雷奇默说。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">接下来，柏林洪堡大学、开姆尼茨工业大学和马尔堡</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">菲利普大学的研究人员希望更详细地研究新化合物的反应性，并将其用于合成新的配合物。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Many chemicals known today are partially or fully aromatic, since a ring framework experiences additional stabilization through the delocalization of π-electrons. While aromatic rings with equal numbers of π-electrons and ring atoms such as benzene are particularly stable, those with the minimally required two π-electrons are very rare and yet remain limited to three- and four-membered rings if not stabilized in the coordination sphere of heavy metals. Here we report the facile synthesis of a dipotassium cyclopentagallene, a unique example of a five-membered aromatic ring stabilized by only two π-electrons. Single-crystal X-ray diffraction revealed a planar Ga<sub>5</sub> ring with almost equal gallium–gallium bond lengths, which together with computational and spectroscopic data confirms its aromatic character. Our results prove that aromatic stabilization goes far beyond what has previously been assumed as minimum π-electron count in a five-atom ring fragment.</span></p>                    <br><br>
