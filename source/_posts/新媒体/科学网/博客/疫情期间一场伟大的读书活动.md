<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="color: #FF0000;"><strong>纳米热力学读书会是一群对<strong>学术研究富有热忱的</strong>理想主义者自发组成的学术研讨组织，一个正在形成中的</strong></span><strong style="white-space: normal; color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">热力学研究的“布尔巴基”学派。</strong></p><p style="text-align: left;">2020年6月，由华盛顿大学的钱纮教授发起，关注热力学和统计物理的基础，特别是纳米热力学和小系统统计物理方面的问题。问题来自物理、生物、化学、数学和统计等方面；研究手段涵盖理论、实验和计算模拟等。报告会基本上两周一次讲座，一般都在周六上午。今年的报告，已经基本安排到了年底。</p><p style="text-align: left;"><span style="color: #FF0000;"><strong>明天是总第65场，由钱纮教授报告。这是2022年度研读书籍的第零讲，研读朗道理论物理教程卷十《物理动理学》的开场演说。</strong></span>所有章节领读人及时间安排参见表格的最后部分。</p><p><span style="color: #FF0000;"></span></p><p>演讲题目：Statistical Kinetics Dictates Energetics: Probability, Entropy, and Possible Geometric Thinking with Symmetry in Statistical Mechanics</p><p>内容提要：Recent studies on stochastic chemical kinetics of counting number of molecules in continuous time have clearly shown that the kinetics dictates Gibbs’ chemical thermodynamics, as a mathematical consequence of the law of large number and its asymptotic entropy function.  The Poisson process figured prominently in our theory.  We now turn our attention to physical kinetics of particles with mechanical movements. The fundamental role of the theory of probability in the portrayal of a system is not merely to provide useful statistical models; it actually is setting up a mathematical space for processing data.  By applying the large deviations theory to stochastic, continuous mechanical motions, Wiener’s  process naturally enters the story, and a novel Boltzmann’s H function arises. The prior probability on the continues time and space becomes a part of  our supposition on space-time geometry. </p><p>人物简介：Professor Hong Qian is Olga Jung Wan Endowed Professor of Applied Mathematics at University of Washington, Seattle. He received his B.A. in Astrophysics from Peking University and Ph.D. in Biochemistry from Washington University in St. Louis, and worked as postdoctoral researcher at University of Oregon and Caltech on biophysical chemistry and mathematical biology. He was elected a fellow of the American Physical Society in 2010. Professor Qian's main research interest is the mathematical approach to and physical understanding of complex systems, especially in terms of stochastic mathematics and statistical physics.  His recent book “Stochastic Chemical Reaction Systems in Biology”, coauthored with H.
