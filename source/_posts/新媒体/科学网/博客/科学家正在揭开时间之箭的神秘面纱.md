Decomposing the local arrow of time in interacting systems. </span><a href="https://journals.aps.org/prl/accepted/0a072Y8eD6a1068ee05096a8ee4316350b055c80c" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">https://journals.aps.org/prl/accepted/0a072Y8eD6a1068ee05096a8ee4316350b055c80c</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">  OnArxiv: </span><a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.14721v1" target="_blank" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">arxiv.org/abs/2112.14721v1</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;">此文帮助解开这个谜。这一发现可能对包括物理学、神经科学和生物学在内的多个学科产生重要影响。</span><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">从根本上说，时间之箭（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/arrow/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">arrow</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"> of </span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/time/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">time</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）源自热力学第二定律：物理系统的微观排列倾向于增加随机性，从有序走向无序。一个系统变得越无序，就越难找到回到有序状态的途径，时间之箭也就越强大。简而言之，宇宙的无序倾向是我们经历时间单向流动的根本原因。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">该论文的第一作者，也是理论科学计划（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-scientists-unraveling-mystery-arrow.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Initiative for the Theoretical Sciences</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>ITS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）项目的博士后研究员克里斯多弗·林恩（</span>Christopher Lynn<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）说：“我们团队面临的两个问题是，如果我们观察一个特定的系统，我们是否能够量化其时间箭头的强度，以及我们是否能够找出它是如何从细胞和神经元（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/neurons/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">neurons</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）相互作用的微观尺度出现到整个系统的？我们的发现为理解我们在日常生活（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/daily+life/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">daily life</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）中经历的时间之箭是如何从这些更微观的细节中浮现出来的迈出了第一步。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">为了回答这些问题，研究人员探索了如何通过观察系统的特定部分及其相互作用来分解时间箭头。例如，这些部分可能是视网膜（</span>retina<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）内起作用的神经元。通过观察一个瞬间，他们发现时间之箭可以分解为不同的部分：由单独工作的部分、成对工作的部分、三元工作的部分或以更复杂的配置产生的部分。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">利用这种分解时间箭头的方法，研究人员分析了现有的关于蝾螈视网膜（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-scientists-unraveling-mystery-arrow.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">salamander retina</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）神经元对不同电影的反应的实验。在一部电影中，一个物体在屏幕上随机移动，而另一部电影则描绘了自然界场景的全部复杂性。研究人员发现，在这两部电影中，时间之箭都来自于神经元对之间的简单互动，而不是大型、复杂的群体。令人惊讶的是，研究小组还观察到，与自然场景相比，观看随机运动时，视网膜显示出更强的时间箭头。克里斯多弗·林恩说，后一个发现提出了一个问题，即我们对时间之箭的内在感知如何与外部世界保持一致。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">克里斯多弗·林恩说：“神经科学研究人员可能对这些结果特别感兴趣。例如，他们可以得出关于时间箭头在神经非典型大脑中的功能是否不同的答案。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">纽约城市大学研究生中心物理学和生物学教授、该研究的主要研究者戴维·施瓦布（</span>David J. Schwab<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）说：“克里斯多弗·林恩对局部不可逆性的分解，也称为时间之箭，是一个优雅的总体框架，可以为探索许多高维非平衡系统提供一个新的视角。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="https://journals.aps.org/prl/accepted/0a072Y8eD6a1068ee05096a8ee4316350b055c80c" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">原文</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">或者</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-scientists-unraveling-mystery-arrow.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">相关报道</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://phys.org/news/2019-07-characterizing-arrow-quantum.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Characterizing the 'arrow of time' in open quantum systems</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://journals.aps.org/prl/accepted/0a072Y8eD6a1068ee05096a8ee4316350b055c80c" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">We show that the evidence for a local arrow of time, which is equivalent to the entropy production in thermodynamic systems, can be decomposed. In a system with many degrees of freedom, there is a term that arises from the irreversible dynamics of the individual variables, and then a series of non–negative terms contributed by correlations among pairs, triplets, and higher–order combinations of variables. We illustrate this decomposition on simple models of noisy logical computations, and then apply it to the analysis of patterns of neural activity in the retina as it responds to complex dynamic visual scenes. We find that neural activity breaks detailed balance even when the visual inputs do not, and that this irreversibility arises primarily from interactions between pairs of neurons.</span></p>                    <br><br>
