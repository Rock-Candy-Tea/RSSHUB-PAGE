font-family: 宋体;">、助理教授</span>Katharina Doblhoff-Dier<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和教授马克·科佩尔（</span>Marc Koper<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）展示了一个新版本。</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">马克·科佩尔的研究团队于</span>2022<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年</span>1<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">月</span>18<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">日在科学期刊《美国国家科学院院刊》（</span></span><a href="https://www.pnas.org/content/119/3/e2116016119" style="text-decoration: underline; font-family: 宋体;">我们必须达到极低浓度的事实告诉我们，双层中的离子比理论预测的要多得多。有一些事情导致了这么多的离子，但理论没有考虑到这一点。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">原文</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">或者</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-01-century-old-electrochemistry-law.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">相关报道</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://www.pnas.org/content/119/3/e2116016119" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Significance</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The Pt(111)–electrolyte interface is one of the most important model interfaces in physical electrochemistry. Here, we present detailed capacitance measurements of Pt(111) in contact with nonspecifically adsorbed electrolytes in combination with modeling to show that this interface does not follow classic Gouy–Chapman–Stern behavior. Our combined experimental-theoretical work suggests a picture of the double layer of Pt(111) with an increased ion concentration close to the interface as a consequence of a weak attractive ion–surface interaction and a changing polarizability of the Pt(111)–water interface due to the potential-dependent water adsorption and orientation.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://www.pnas.org/content/119/3/e2116016119" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">We present detailed measurements of the double-layer capacitance of the Pt(111)–electrolyte interface close to the potential of zero charge (PZC) in the presence of several different electrolytes consisting of anions and cations that are considered to be nonspecifically adsorbed. For low electrolyte concentrations, we show strong deviations from traditional Gouy–Chapman–Stern (GCS) behavior that appear to be independent of the nature of the electrolyte ions. Focusing on the capacitance further away from PZC and the trends for increasing ion concentration, we observe ion-specific capacitance effects that appear to be related to the size or hydration strength of the ions. We formulate a model for the structure of the electric double layer of the Pt(111)–electrolyte interface that goes significantly beyond the GCS theory. By combining two existing models, namely, one capturing the water reorganization on Pt close to the PZC and one accounting for an attractive ion–surface interaction not included in the GCS model, we can reproduce and interpret the main features the experimental capacitance of the Pt(111)–electrolyte interface. The model suggests a picture of the double layer with an increased ion concentration close to the interface as a consequence of a weak attractive ion–surface interaction, and a changing polarizability of the Pt(111)–water interface due to the potential-dependent water adsorption and orientation.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
