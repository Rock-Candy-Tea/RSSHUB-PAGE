font-family: 宋体;">）的研究人员。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">何为“永久化学品（</span>Forever chemicals<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）”或者“永久性化学物质”？所谓“永久性化学品”实际上是指包含至少一个完全氟化碳原子的一类人工合成的有机氟化合物的总称。或者是指全氟和多氟烷基物质（</span></span><a href="https://www.niehs.nih.gov/health/topics/agents/pfc/index.cfm" target="_blank" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Perfluoroalkyl and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>PFAS</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">），因为这些化合物在自然条件下难以分解，具有优良的化学稳定性、热稳定性，疏水、疏油且表面活性高，还被广泛应用于消防泡沫、不粘材料、涂料、造纸、纺织及其他行业中。并可以在食品包装、电子产品、化妆品、炊具等日常用品中找到，而且研究发现，现在这些化学品在全球各地的雨水中都有，甚至包括南极洲。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，一组自</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">世纪</span>40<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年代以来常用的人造化学品，之所以被称为“永久性化学品”，这是有原因的。细菌不能吃掉它们；火不能把它们烧成灰烬；水不能稀释它们。而且，如果这些有毒化学物质被掩埋，它们会渗入周围的土壤，成为子孙后代的一个持久问题。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">现在，美中化学家们已经完成了看似不可能的任务。利用低温和廉价的普通试剂，研究团队开发了一种工艺，使两大类</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">化合物分解，只留下良性的最终产物。这项简单的技术可能是最终处理这些有害化学物质的有力解决方案，这些化学物质会对人类、牲畜和环境造成许多危险的健康影响。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">美国西北大学的威廉·迪克特尔</span>(</span><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abm8868#con6" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">William R. Dichtel</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">领导了这项研究，他说：“</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">已经成为一个主要的社会问题。即使是极少量的</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">也会对健康造成负面影响，而且不会分解。我们不能对此袖手旁观，坐等这个问题解决。我们想用化学方法来解决这个问题，并创造出一个全世界都可以使用的解决方案。这很令人兴奋，因为我们的解决方案是如此简单，但尚未得到认可。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">威廉·迪克特尔是美国西北大学温伯格文理学院（</span>Northwestern's Weinberg College of Arts and Sciences<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的罗伯特·雷特辛格（</span>Robert L. Letsinger<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）化学教授。这篇论文的共同第一作者之一布列塔妮·庄（</span></span><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abm8868#con1" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Brittany Trang</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">），最近在威廉·迪克特尔实验室完成了一篇博士论文，并将该项目作为其中的一部分进行了研究。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“与铅同类”（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-chemicals-simple-method.html" target="_blank">'The same category as lead'</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">作为不粘和防水剂已经使用了</span>70<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年。它们通常存在于不粘炊具、防水化妆品、消防泡沫、拒水织物和抗油脂产品中。然而，多年来，</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">已经从消费品中脱颖而出，进入了我们的饮用水，甚至进入了</span>97%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的美国人口的血液。尽管对健康的影响尚未完全了解，但</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">暴露与生育能力下降、儿童发育影响、各类癌症风险增加、抗感染免疫力降低和胆固醇水平升高密切相关。考虑到这些不利的健康影响，美国环境保护署（</span>U.S. Environmental Protection Agency<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>EPA<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">牢不可破的化学键（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-chemicals-simple-method.html" target="_blank">Unbreakable bonds</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">尽管学术界研究者已经从水中滤出</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的努力取得了成功，但一旦</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">被去除，如何处置它的解决方案却寥寥无几。目前出现的少数选择通常涉及在高温和高压下销毁</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">或其他需要大量能量输入的方法。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">威廉·迪克特尔说：“在美国纽约州，一家声称焚烧全氟辛烷磺酸的工厂被发现将其中一些化合物释放到空气中。这些化合物是从烟囱排放到当地社区的。另一个失败的策略是将此类化合物掩埋在垃圾填埋场。这仅仅是权宜之计，尚未从根本上解决问题。当你这样做的时候，你无法保证在</span>30<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年后不会出现问题，是因为它会慢慢渗出，污染周围环境。此举无异于是掩耳盗铃，自欺欺人，根本没有解决问题。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">坚不可摧的秘密在于它的化学键。</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">含有许多碳氟键（</span>C<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">—</span>F<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），它们是有机化学（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/organic+chemistry/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">organic chemistry</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）中最强的键。氟作为元素周期表中电负性最强的元素，非常需要电子。另一方面，碳更愿意放弃它的电子。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">威廉·迪克特尔解释道：“当两个原子之间存在这种差异，并且它们的大小大致相同时，这就是碳和氟之间形成真正强键的原因所在。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">精确定位</span>PFAS</strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的“致命弱点”（</span> </strong><strong><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-chemicals-simple-method.html" target="_blank">Pinpointing PFAS' Achilles' heel</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">但在研究这些化合物时，威廉·迪克特尔的团队发现了一个弱点。</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">包含一条不屈服的碳</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">氟键长尾。但在分子的一端，有一个带电基团，通常含有带电的氧原子。威廉·迪克特尔的团队通过在二甲基亚砜（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/dimethyl+sulfoxide/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">dimethyl sulfoxide</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）中加热</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">来瞄准这一头基（</span>head group<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。二甲基亚砜是一种用氢氧化钠（一种常用试剂）破坏</span>PFAS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的特殊溶剂。这一过程将头基斩首，留下一条反应性尾巴。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">威廉·迪克特尔说：“这引发了所有这些反应，它开始从这些化合物中吐出氟原子，形成氟化物，这是氟的最安全形式。尽管碳</span>-<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">氟键非常强，但这个带电的头基团是致命的弱点（</span>Achilles' heel<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) have been referred to as “forever chemicals” because of their resistance to most biological and chemical degradation mechanisms. Most current methods use very harsh conditions to decompose these compounds. Trang <em>et al</em>. found that there is a potential weak spot in carboxylic acid–containing PFAS: Decarboxylation in polar, non-protic solvents yields a carbanion that rapidly decomposes (see the Perspective by Joudan and Lundgren). The authors used computational work and experiments to show that this process involves fluoride elimination, hydroxide addition, and carbon–carbon bond scission. The initial decarboxylation step is rate limiting, and subsequent defluorination and chain shortening steps occur through a series of low barrier steps. The procedure can accommodate perfluoroether carboxylic acids, although sulfonic acids are not currently compatible. —MAF</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abm8868" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) are persistent, bioaccumulative pollutants found in water resources at concentrations harmful to human health. Whereas current PFAS destruction strategies use nonselective destruction mechanisms, we found that perfluoroalkyl carboxylic acids (PFCAs) could be mineralized through a sodium hydroxide–mediated defluorination pathway. PFCA decarboxylation in polar aprotic solvents produced reactive perfluoroalkyl ion intermediates that degraded to fluoride ions (78 to ~100%) within 24 hours. The carbon-containing intermediates and products were inconsistent with oft-proposed one-carbon-chain shortening mechanisms, and we instead computationally identified pathways consistent with many experiments. Degradation was also observed for branched perfluoroalkyl ether carboxylic acids and might be extended to degrade other PFAS classes as methods to activate their polar headgroups are identified.</span></p>                    <br><br>
