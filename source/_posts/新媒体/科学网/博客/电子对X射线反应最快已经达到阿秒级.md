font-family: "times new roman";">Charge transfer plays a fundamental role in many chemical and biological processes, yet many important questions about its mechanics at the electronic level remain unanswered. Recent development of attosecond x-ray free-electron laser sources enabled site-specific valence excitations in molecular systems on a time scale shorter than the natural charge dynamics. Li et al. use this technology to produce coherent superpositions of core-excited states in the model molecule nitric oxide, which is composed of biorelevant atoms.
