Taube <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">说：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">接下来是重要的步骤。我们需要多机构研究表明这些测试可以标准化，然后进行前瞻性临床试验，将</span>AstroPath<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的下一代诊断潜力带到患者护理中。</span>”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">除了开发新的伴随诊断外，该团队的长期目标还包括构建肿瘤免疫图谱的</span></span><span style="font-size: 18px;">开源图谱</span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">（</span>open-source atlas<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），类似于美国国家癌症研究所的癌症基因组图谱。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">马克癌症研究基金会（</span>Mark Foundation for Cancer Research<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）首席执行官</span> Michele Cleary <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">说：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">天文学中先进绘图技术的应用有可能识别出预测性生物标志物，这些生物标志物将帮助医生为个体癌症患者设计精确的免疫疗法。这些早期结果令人兴奋并验证了该方法，我们马克癌症研究基金会很自豪能够支持这种开创性的科学研究。</span>”上述介绍仅供参考，欲了解更多信息敬请注意浏览<a href="https://science.sciencemag.org/content/372/6547/eaba2609" target="_blank">原文</a>或者<a href="https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2021/06/210610150116.htm" target="_blank">相关报道</a>。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://science.sciencemag.org/content/372/6547/eaba2609" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Astronomy accelerates tumor imaging</span></a></p><p style="margin: 3px 0px 10px; background: rgb(230, 230, 230); line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Immunohistochemical stains for individual markers revolutionized diagnostic pathology decades ago but cannot capture enough information to accurately predict response to immunotherapy. Newer multiplex immunofluorescent technologies provide the potential to visualize the expression patterns of many functionally relevant molecules but present numerous challenges in accurate image analysis and data handling, particularly over large tumor areas. Drawing from the field of astronomy, in which petabytes of imaging data are routinely analyzed across a wide spectral range, Berry <em>et al.</em> developed a platform for multispectral imaging of whole-tumor sections with high-fidelity single-cell resolution. The resultant AstroPath platform was used to develop a multiplex immunofluorescent assay highly predictive of responses and outcomes for melanoma patients receiving immunotherapy.</span></p><p style="margin: 10px 0px 0px; background: rgb(230, 230, 230); line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><em><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 14px;">Science</span></em><span style="font-size: 14px; font-family: "Times New Roman";">, aba2609, this issue p. </span><a href="https://science.sciencemag.org/lookup/doi/10.1126/science.aba2609" target="_blank"><strong><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px; color: #37588A;">eaba2609</span></strong></a></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://science.sciencemag.org/content/372/6547/eaba2609" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Structured Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">INTRODUCTION</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0px; background: rgb(230, 230, 230); line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">New therapies have been designed to stimulate the host’s immune system to fight cancer. Despite these exciting, recent successes, a large proportion of patients still do not respond to anti–programmed cell death-1 (PD-1) or anti–programmed death ligand-1 (PD-L1) therapies, and thus, biomarkers for patient selection are highly desirable. The only U.S. Food and Drug Administration–approved histopathology biomarker tests for anti–PD-1 or anti–PD-L1 therapy is assessment of PD-L1 protein expression by means of immunohistochemistry. This approach is unidimensional and has limitations. Innovative characterization of the tumor microenvironment (TME) with a focus on multidimensional, spatially resolved interactions at the single-cell level will provide critical mechanistic insights into therapeutic responses and potentially identify improved biomarkers for patient selection. Using multispectral approaches to image the TME and substituting cells for stars and galaxies, we applied the methodology and infrastructure developed for astronomy to pathologic analysis of specimens from patients with melanoma.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">RATIONALE</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0px; background: rgb(230, 230, 230); line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The next generation of pathologic analyses will require platforms that can characterize the coexpression of key molecules on specific cellular subsets in situ and spatial relationships between tumor cells and multiple immune elements. To that aim, we applied astronomical algorithms for high-quality imaging and the establishment of relational databases to multiplex immunofluorescence (mIF) labeling of pathology specimens, facilitating spatial analyses and immunoarchitectural characterization of the host-tumor interface. In all, we curated and coordinately mapped six markers, both individually and in combination in tumor tissue from 98 patients with melanoma receiving anti–PD-1 therapy. This dataset comprised ~127,400 image mosaics composed of more than 100 million single cells. The data outputs were linked to patient outcomes, informing in a clinically relevant way how cancer evades the immune system and potentiating biomarker assay development for precision immunotherapy.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">RESULTS</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0px; background: rgb(230, 230, 230); line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The imaging protocols used in this study were used to address outstanding questions regarding the impact of high-power field sampling strategies on biomarker performance. This information was then used to develop an approach for operator-independent field selection. The image handling strategies also facilitated the robust assessment of the intensity of PD-1 and PD-L1 expression in situ (negative, low, mid, and high levels) on different cell types. Thus, with only six markers (PD-1, PD-L1, CD8, FoxP3, CD163, and Sox10/S100), we were able to develop 41 combinations of expression patterns for these molecules and map relatively rare cells such as CD8<sup>+</sup>FoxP3<sup>+</sup> cells to the tumor stromal boundary. Moreover, a high density of CD8<sup>+</sup>FoxP3<sup>+</sup>PD-1<sup>low/mid</sup> cells was closely associated with response to PD-1 blockade. Cell types associated with a lack of response to therapy were also identified—for example, CD163<sup>+</sup> macrophages that were PD-L1<sup>–</sup>. This latter phenotype was also found to have a negative effect on long-term survival. When these and other key cell phenotype densities were combined, they were highly predictive of objective response and stratified long-term patient outcomes after anti–PD-1–based therapies in both a discovery cohort and an independent validation cohort.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">CONCLUSION</span></p><p style="margin: 0px 0px 10px; background: rgb(230, 230, 230); line-height: 2em;"><span style="color: #333333; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Here, we present the AstroPath platform, an end-to-end pathology workflow with rigorous quality control for creating quantitative, spatially resolved mIF datasets. Although the current effort focused on a six-plex mIF assay, the principles described here provide a general framework for the development of any multiplex assay with single-cell image resolution. Such approaches will vastly improve the standardization and scalability of these technologies, enabling cross-site and cross-study comparisons. This will be essential for multiplex imaging technologies to realize their potential as biomarker discovery platforms and ultimately as standard diagnostic tests for clinical therapeutic decision-making.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"> </span></p>                    <br><br>
