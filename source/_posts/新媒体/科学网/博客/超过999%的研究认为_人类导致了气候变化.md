font-family: 宋体;">的同行评议科学论文同意，气候变化主要是由人类造成的。这项研究更新了</span>2013<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年的一篇类似论文，该论文显示，</span>1991-2012<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年间发表的</span>97%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的研究支持人类活动正在改变地球气候的观点。目前的调查研究了从</span>2012<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年到</span>2020<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年</span>11<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">月发表的文献，以探索共识是否已经改变。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">康奈尔大学科学联盟</span>(Alliance for Science at Cornell University)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）说：“认识到温室气体排放的主要作用至关重要，这样我们才能迅速动员新的解决方案，因为我们已经亲眼目睹了气候相关灾害对企业、人民和经济的毁灭性影响。在同行评议的科学文献中，对人类造成气候变化的共识超过</span>99%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">尽管有这样的结果，民意调查以及政治家和公众代表的意见都指向错误的信念和主张，即科学家们对气候变化的真正原因仍存在重大争论。</span>2016<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年，皮尤研究中心</span>(Pew Research Center)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">发现，只有</span>27%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的美国成年人认为“几乎所有”科学家都认为气候变化是人类活动造成的。</span>2021<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年盖洛普</span>(Gallup)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的一项民意调查显示，在自工业革命以来观测到的地球气温上升是否主要是由人类造成的问题上，美国政界的党派分歧正在加深。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">马克·莱纳斯说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“要理解共识存在于何处，你必须能够量化它。这意味着以连贯而非武断的方式调查文献，以避免交易精心挑选的论文，而这往往是这些争论在公共领域进行的方式。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在这项研究中，研究人员从</span>2012-2020<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年发表的</span>88125<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">篇英语气候论文中随机抽取了</span>3000<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">篇研究。他们发现，在</span>3000<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">篇论文中，只有</span>4<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">篇对人类造成的气候变化持怀疑态度。马克·莱纳斯说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“我们知道</span>(<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">气候怀疑论者的论文</span>)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">发生的次数微乎其微，但我们认为在</span>8.8<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">万多篇论文中肯定还有更多。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">合著者西蒙·佩里</span>(Simon Perry)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">是一名在英国工作的软件工程师，也是科学联盟</span>(Alliance for Science)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的志愿者。他创建了一种算法，可以从此团队知道存在怀疑的论文中搜索关键字，比如“太阳（</span>solar<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）”、“宇宙射线（</span>cosmic rays<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）”和“自然周期（</span>natural cycles<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）”。该算法应用于所有</span>88000<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">多篇论文，程序对它们进行排序，因此怀疑者的排名更高。正如预期的那样，他们发现许多持不同意见的论文都排在了前面，而收益递减（</span>diminishing returns<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的论文则排在后面。总的来说，这项研究产生了</span>28<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">篇含蓄或明确持怀疑态度的论文，都发表在次要期刊上。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">西蒙·莱纳斯说，如果</span>2013<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年的研究</span>97%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的结果仍然让人们对人类对气候的影响的科学共识存在一些疑问，那么当前的研究结果进一步消除了任何不确定性。“这应该是最后的定论了，”他说。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1748-9326/ac2966" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">原文</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">或者<a href="https://news.cornell.edu/stories/2021/10/more-999-studies-agree-humans-caused-climate-change" target="_self">相关报道</a>。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1748-9326/ac2966" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">While controls over the Earth's climate system have undergone rigorous hypothesis-testing since the 1800s, questions over the scientific consensus of the role of human activities in modern climate change continue to arise in public settings. We update previous efforts to quantify the scientific consensus on climate change by searching the recent literature for papers sceptical of anthropogenic-caused global warming. From a dataset of 88125 climate-related papers published since 2012, when this question was last addressed comprehensively, we examine a randomized subset of 3000 such publications. We also use a second sample-weighted approach that was specifically biased with keywords to help identify any sceptical peer-reviewed papers in the whole dataset. We identify four sceptical papers out of the sub-set of 3000, as evidenced by abstracts that were rated as implicitly or explicitly sceptical of human-caused global warming. In our sample utilizing pre-identified sceptical keywords we found 28 papers that were implicitly or explicitly sceptical. We conclude with high statistical confidence that the scientific consensus on human-caused contemporary climate change—expressed as a proportion of the total publications—exceeds 99% in the peer reviewed scientific literature.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
