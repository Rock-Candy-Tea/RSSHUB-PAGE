Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, June 9, 2022; 119 (24): e2200830119. </span><a href="https://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2200830119" target="_blank" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2200830119</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">. </span><a href="https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2200830119" target="_blank" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2200830119</a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">几千年来，人类已经知道铜</span>(</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/copper/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">copper</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的抗菌特性。例如，古埃及人（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-06-wearable-antimicrobial-copper-nanomesh-human.html" target="_blank">Ancient Egyptians</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）用它治疗伤口以防止感染。在现代，铜被用于家庭和医院中的柜台和桌面，以杀死细菌。在这项新的研究中，研究人员采用了一种新的方法来使用铜来对抗感染：使铜几乎看不见。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">铜很重，用作抗菌材料时会很难看。为了使其更易于使用，研究人员创造了微小的铜绞线，并将其随机纺在一起，形成一个网格。然后，他们施加压力使网格变平。在<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">3</span>微米（</span>3 μm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）厚的情况下，其结果是如此之薄，以至于肉眼无法看到或触摸时无法感觉到。但它是可弯曲和有弹性的，这意味着它可以用多种方式杀死病毒和细菌。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">研究人员预计纳米网的主要应用之一是作为智能手机和平板电脑的表面覆盖物。测试表明，纳米网不会影响此类设备的性能。目标是将网格应用于作为细菌和病毒转移点的表面，例如门把手、电灯开关和用于制作衣服的材料内部。一种可能性是，一个薄到用户不知道它的存在的涂层手套可能会提供最好的保护，因为这么多的微生物是通过手传递的。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">研究人员表示，他们的纳米网优于已经在使用的铜膜，因为它更有效。纳米网具有更大的表面积（</span>more </span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/surface+area/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">surface area</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">），使其更有机会杀死微生物。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Copper, but not silver, is effective against SARS-CoV-2 on surfaces</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2200830119" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Significance</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The recent COVID-19 pandemic requires long-term and real-life applicable antimicrobial skin protection. However, there has been no practical solution to prevent cross-infection while preserving intrinsic skin naturalness. Conventional blocking-based approaches such as gloves cannot preserve the skin sterility and modify the morphology, temperature change rate, and humidity affecting our sensation and comfort. Here, we propose a skin-attachable protection platform copper nanomesh, which prevents cross-infection while maintaining skin naturalness. Copper nanomesh composed of copper coating and interconnected polymer nanofibers kills 99.99% of bacteria and viruses within 1 and 10 min and prevents bacterial cross-infection. The thin and porous structure of the nanomesh enables natural skin-environment interaction in terms of the morphology, temperature change rate, and humidity compared to films or gloves.</span></p><p><a href="https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2200830119" target="_blank" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</a><br></p><section><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The functional support and advancement of our body while preserving inherent naturalness is one of the ultimate goals of bioengineering. Skin protection against infectious pathogens is an application that requires common and long-term wear without discomfort or distortion of the skin functions. However, no antimicrobial method has been introduced to prevent cross-infection while preserving intrinsic skin conditions. Here, we propose an antimicrobial skin protection platform copper nanomesh, which prevents cross-infectionmorphology, temperature change rate, and skin humidity. Copper nanomesh exhibited an inactivation rate of 99.99% for <em style="box-sizing: border-box">Escherichia coli</em> bacteria and influenza virus A within 1 and 10 min, respectively. The thin and porous nanomesh allows for conformal coating on the fingertips, without significant interference with the rate of skin temperature change and humidity. Efficient cross-infection prevention and thermal transfer of copper nanomesh were demonstrated using direct on-hand experiments.</span></p></section><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
