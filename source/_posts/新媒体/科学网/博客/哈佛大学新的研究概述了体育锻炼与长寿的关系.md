font-family: 宋体;">）的研究人员合作完成的新研究，概述了体育锻炼与长寿的关系（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-outlines-longer-tied-physical.html" target="_self">New research outlines how longer lives are tied to physical activity<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。相关研究结果于</span>2021<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年</span>11<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">月</span>14<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">日已经在《美国国家科学院院刊》（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/journals/proceedings-of-the-national-academy-of-sciences/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）杂志网站发表——</span>Daniel E. Lieberman, Timothy M. Kistner, Daniel Richard, I-Min Lee, Aaron L. Baggish. The active grandparent hypothesis: Physical activity and the evolution of extended human healthspans and lifespans. </span><a href="https://phys.org/journals/proceedings-of-the-national-academy-of-sciences/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，</span>December 14, 2021, 118(50): e2107621118.  </span><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2107621118" target="_blank" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2107621118</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">. </span><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2107621118" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">http://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2107621118</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">几乎每个人都知道运动对你有好处。众所周知，生命在于运动。有些人甚至能快速说出它保持肌肉和关节强壮的原因，以及它是如何对抗某些疾病的。但有多少人能告诉你为什么以及如何将体育活动纳入人类生物学（</span>human biology<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）？</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">一个由哈佛大学进化生物学家和生物医学研究人员组成的团队在《美国国家科学院院刊》</span>(PNAS)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">上发表的一项新研究中对此进行了研究。这项研究列出了进化和生物医学方面的证据，这些证据表明，在停止生育后进化还能活几十年的人类，在他们的晚年也进化得相对活跃。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">研究人员表示，晚年的身体活动会将能量从损害健康的过程中转移到延长健康的身体机制中。他们假设，随着年龄的增长，人类在进化过程中保持身体活跃，这样做是为了将能量分配到生理过程中，从而减缓身体的逐渐退化。这可以预防慢性疾病，如心血管疾病、</span>2<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">型糖尿病，甚至一些癌症。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">该论文的主要作者、哈佛大学进化生物学家丹尼尔·利伯曼</span>(Daniel E. Lieberman)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">说：“在西方社会，人们普遍认为，随着年龄的增长，放慢脚步、少做点事、然后退休是正常的。而我们的观点恰恰相反</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">随着年龄的增长，保持体育锻炼变得更加重要。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">包括来自哈佛医学院的</span>Aaron Baggish<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和</span>I-Min Lee<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在内的研究团队认为，这篇论文首次详细解释了为什么随着人类年龄增长，缺乏体育锻炼（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/lack+of+physical+activity/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">lack of physical activity</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）会增加患病风险，并缩短寿命。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">47<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁的亚伦·巴吉胥（</span>Aaron L. Baggish<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）还是新英格兰爱国者队</span>(New England Patriots)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和美国足球队</span>(U.S. Soccer)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的心脏病专家，他和</span>57<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁的丹尼尔·利伯曼是长期的跑步伙伴，他们经常在</span>5<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">到</span>10<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">英里的晨跑中讨论那些见报的想法。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这项研究以人类的类人猿亲缘（</span>humans' ape cousins<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）为切入点。研究人员指出，猿类在野外通常只能活</span>35-40<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年，而且很少能活过更年期（</span>past menopause<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），它们的活跃度比大多数人类要低得多，这表明人类进化中存在着选择，不仅是为了活得更长，也为了更活跃的身体。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">丹尼尔·利伯曼说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“我们基本上是从电视迷（</span>couch potatoes<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）进化而来的。”丹尼尔·利伯曼他曾两次在坦桑尼亚观察野生黑猩猩（</span>chimpanzees in Tanzania<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），对它们一天中有多少时间是“坐着消化（</span>sitting on their butts, digesting<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）”感到惊讶。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">与当代的狩猎采集者（</span>hunter-gatherers<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）相比，这一点尤为突出。当代的狩猎采集者平均每天进行</span>135<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">分钟的中到高强度体力活动。这种运动水平大约是美国人体力活动平均水平的</span>6<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">到</span>10<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">倍，这可能是为什么童年时期幸存的狩猎采集者往往能活到</span>70<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁的关键之一，比人类一般停止生育的年龄大约多</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岁。化石证据表明，早在</span>4<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">万年前，人类的寿命就普遍延长了，这与此前认为人类寿命很短的观点相反。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">该团队强调，体育活动对健康的主要好处是延长人类的健康寿命，即健康状态良好的生命年限。研究人员研究了终身体育活动重新分配能量以改善健康状况的两种途径。第一种是将多余的能量从潜在的有害机制中释放出来，比如多余的脂肪储存。该团队还确定了身体活动如何分配能量来修复和维护过程。这篇论文表明，除了燃烧卡路里外，体育活动是一种生理压力，会在分子、细胞和组织水平上对身体造成损害。然而，身体对这种伤害的反应本质上是重新变得更强。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这包括修复肌肉纤维的撕裂，修复软骨损伤和愈合微骨折。这种反应还会导致运动相关的抗氧化剂和消炎物质的释放，并促进血液流动。在缺乏体力活动的情况下，这些反应被激活的次数较少。细胞和</span>DNA<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">修复过程已被证明可以降低糖尿病、肥胖、癌症、骨质疏松、老年痴呆症和抑郁症的风险。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">丹尼尔·利伯曼说</span>: <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“关键的要点是，因为我们在一生中进化得很活跃，我们的身体需要体育活动来健康地老去。在过去，为了生存，每天的体育活动是必要的，但今天我们不得不选择锻炼，也就是为了健康和健身而自愿参加体育活动。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">包括研究生蒂莫西·基斯特纳</span>(Timothy Kistner)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和丹尼尔·理查德</span>(Daniel Richard)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在内的研究团队希望，这项研究能让这一信息更难被忽视。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">随着机器和技术取代人类劳动，世界范围内的体力活动水平一直在下降。丹尼尔·利伯曼实验室最近的一项研究表明，美国人的身体活动比</span>200<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年前少了。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">研究者的建议你离开椅子，做些运动。丹尼尔·利伯曼说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“关键是去做一些事情，并努力让它变得有趣，这样你就会一直做下去。好消息是，你不需要像狩猎采集者那样活跃。即使是每天</span>10<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">或</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">分钟的少量体育活动（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/physical+activity/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">physical activity</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）也会大大降低你的死亡风险。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2107621118" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">原文</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">或者<a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-outlines-longer-tied-physical.html" target="_self">相关报道</a>。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2107621118" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The proximate mechanisms by which physical activity (PA) slows senescence and decreases morbidity and mortality have been extensively documented. However, we lack an ultimate, evolutionary explanation for why lifelong PA, particularly during middle and older age, promotes health. As the growing worldwide epidemic of physical inactivity accelerates the prevalence of noncommunicable diseases among aging populations, integrating evolutionary and biomedical perspectives can foster new insights into how and why lifelong PA helps preserve health and extend lifespans. Building on previous life-history research, we assess the evidence that humans were selected not just to live several decades after they cease reproducing but also to be moderately physically active during those postreproductive years. We next review the longstanding hypothesis that PA promotes health by allocating energy away from potentially harmful overinvestments in fat storage and reproductive tissues and propose the novel hypothesis that PA also stimulates energy allocation toward repair and maintenance processes. We hypothesize that selection in humans for lifelong PA, including during postreproductive years to provision offspring, promoted selection for both energy allocation pathways which synergistically slow senescence and reduce vulnerability to many forms of chronic diseases. As a result, extended human healthspans and lifespans are both a cause and an effect of habitual PA, helping explain why lack of lifelong PA in humans can increase disease risk and reduce longevity.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
