font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">为了使聚变能成为世界能源网的可行资源，研究人员需要了解等离子体的湍流运动。等离子体是在反应堆容器中旋转的离子和电子的混合物。等离子体粒子在被称为托卡马克（</span>tokamaks<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的环形室跟随磁场线之后</span>,<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">必须受足够长的时间融合设备限制以产生净能量显著提升</span>,<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">但当等离子体的高温边缘</span>(<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">超过</span>100<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">万℃</span>)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">距离容器的较冷固体壁只有几厘米远时，这就是一个挑战。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">麻省理工学院等离子体科学与聚变中心</span>(MIT's Plasma Science and Fusion Center<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>MIT's PSFC)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">工作的核科学与工程系博士生</span>Abhilash Mathews<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">认为，等离子体边缘是一个特别丰富的未解问题的来源。湍流边界（</span>turbulent boundary<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）对理解等离子体限制、供给燃料和可能撞击材料表面的潜在破坏性热通量，这些影响聚变反应堆设计的因素都是极为重要的。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">为了更好地理解边缘条件，科学家们专注于利用数值模拟来模拟这一边界处的湍流，这将有助于预测等离子体的行为。然而，该区域的“第一原理”模拟是聚变研究中最具挑战性和耗时的计算之一。如果研究人员能够开发出运行速度更快，且具有量化准确性的“简化”计算机模型，那么其研究进展将会加快。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">几十年来，托卡马克物理学家（</span>tokamak physicists<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）在实验中经常使用简化的“双流体理论（</span>two-fluid theory<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）”，而不是高保真度模型来模拟边界等离子体，尽管准确性存在不确定性。在最近发表的两篇文章中，</span>Abhilash Mathews<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">开始用一种新方法直接测试这个简化的等离子体湍流模型的准确性</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">他将物理和机器学习结合起来。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Abhilash Mathews<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">解释说：“一个成功的理论应该能够预测你将要观察到的东西，如温度、密度、电势及流量等。正是这些变量之间的关系从根本上定义了湍流理论。我们的工作本质上是研究这两个变量即湍流电场和电子压力之间的动态关系。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">2021<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年</span>8<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">月</span>13<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">日发表于《物理评论</span>E<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">》（</span></span><a href="https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; Physical Review E, 2021, 104(2): 025205 – Published 13 August 2021. DOI: </span><a href="https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">. </span><a href="https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">.<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">就是采用了一种新的深度学习技术，使用人工神经网络来建立控制简化流体理论的方程表示。在这个框架下，</span>Abhilash Mathews<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">演示了一种计算等离子体中电子压力波动湍流电场的方法，该方法与简化流体理论一致。通常用来将电场与压力联系起来的模型，在应用于湍流等离子体时就会失效，但这个模型即使在噪声压力测量中也很可靠。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">2021<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年</span>11<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">月初发表在《等离子体物理学》（</span></span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0066064" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Physics of Plasmas</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）上的第二篇论文——</span><a href="https://aip.scitation.org/author/Mathews%2C+A" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"> A. Francisquez</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">, </span><a href="https://aip.scitation.org/author/Hughes%2C+J+W" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">J. W. Abhilash Mathews<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">作为第一作者，进一步研究了这种联系，并将其与高保真度湍流模拟进行了对比。这种史无前例的对不同模型间湍流的比较，以前很难精确评估。</span>Abhilash Mathews<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">发现，在与现有聚变装置相关的等离子体中，简化流体模型预测的湍流场与高保真计算一致。从这个意义上说，简化湍流理论是可行的。但要完全验证它，“应该检查每个变量之间的每个连接，”</span>Abhilash Mathews<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">指出，等离子体湍流是出了名的难以模拟，比我们在空气和水中看到的湍流还要难。“这项工作表明，在适当的条件下，基于物理的机器学习技术可以从有限的一组观察开始，描绘出快速波动的边缘等离子体的非常完整的画面。我很高兴看到我们可以把它应用到新的实验中，在这些实验中，我们从来没有观察到我们想要的每个量。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的工作是一项具有广泛应用潜力的重大成就。例如，给定特定等离子体量的有限诊断测量，物理信息机器学习（</span>physics-informed machine learning<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）可以推断出附近区域的额外等离子体量，从而增强给定诊断提供的信息。该技术也为模型验证开辟了新的策略。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; Mathews et al, Uncovering turbulent plasma dynamics via deep learning from partial observations, Physical Review E (2021). DOI: </span><a href="https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.104.025205" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">One of the most intensely studied aspects of magnetic confinement fusion is edge plasma turbulence which is critical to reactor performance and operation. Drift-reduced Braginskii two-fluid theory has for decades been widely applied to model boundary plasmas with varying success. Towards better understanding edge turbulence in both theory and experiment, we demonstrate that a physics-informed deep learning framework constrained by partial differential equations can accurately learn turbulent fields consistent with the two-fluid theory from partial observations of electron pressure which is not otherwise possible using conventional equilibrium models. This technique presents a paradigm for the advanced design of plasma diagnostics and validation of magnetized plasma turbulence theories in challenging thermonuclear environments.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">A. DOI: </span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0066064" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">10.1063/5.0066064</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">. Published Online: 01 November 2021. </span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0066064" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0066064</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0066064" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">A key uncertainty in the design and development of magnetic confinement fusion energy reactors is predicting edge plasma turbulence. An essential step in overcoming this uncertainty is the validation in accuracy of reduced turbulent transport models. Drift-reduced Braginskii two-fluid theory is one such set of reduced equations that has for decades simulated boundary plasmas in experiment, but significant questions exist regarding its predictive ability. To this end, using a novel physics-informed deep learning framework, we demonstrate the first ever direct quantitative comparisons of turbulent field fluctuations between electrostatic two-fluid theory and electromagnetic gyrokinetic modeling with good overall agreement found in magnetized helical plasmas at low normalized pressure. This framework presents a new technique for the numerical validation and discovery of reduced global plasma turbulence models.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
