font-family: 宋体;">秒。在向媒体发表的声明中，他们指出他们的成就是保持过热等离子体的新记录。详见</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-01-chinese-tokamak-facility-million-degree-seconds.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，</span>Jan 05, 2022.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">这项工作致力于开发聚变作为一种</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/power+source/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">能源</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">，可以替代燃煤</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/power+plants/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">发电厂</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，也可能替代其他可再生资源发电厂。基本思想是模仿太阳，它通过聚变产生光和热。当加热到高温时，振动的原子变得非常兴奋，以至于它们的原子核融合在一起。在太阳中，所涉及的原子是氢。当它们融合在一起时，就会形成氦</span>-4<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">（</span><sup>4</sup>He<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），一种比两个</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/hydrogen+atoms/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">氢原子</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">还轻的元素。质量差以</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/thermal+energy/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">热能形式释放</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">，它以光和热的形式从太阳中释放出来。多年来，科学家们一直致力于在托卡马克等设施（</span><a href="http://english.ipp.cas.cn/syxw/202112/t20211231_295485.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">tokamak facility</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）中复制这一过程。在这样的设施中，少量原子被微波加热到非常高的温度，产生等离子体，并通过用磁铁将它们固定在适当的位置，并用可以承受的材料衬里周围的材料来防止燃烧包含它们的甜甜圈形设施非常高的温度，例如碳。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在托卡马克</span>(tokamak)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">中，氚</span>(tritium, <sup>3</sup>H)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和氘</span>(deuterium, <sup>2</sup>H)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">原子被加热到非常</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/high+temperatures/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">高的温度</span>——</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">直到它们开始融合的温度</span>——<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">大约</span>1.5<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">亿℃。但这样做只是过程中的一个步骤。对于持续产生能量的设施，</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/fusion+reactions/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">聚变反应</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">必须是自我维持的，就像太阳一样。因此，像托卡马克设施的团队一直在努力制作越来越长的反应场景。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在他们的公告中，该团队指出，他们的结果表明他们正在朝着目标稳步前进。</span>2021<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年年初，他们将等离子体样本在</span>1.2<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">亿℃的温度下保持了</span> 101 <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">秒。他们还表示，他们的反应堆旨在尽可能地复制太阳中的聚变过程。他们将他们的设施称为</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">人造太阳（</span>artificial sun<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span>”<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-01-chinese-tokamak-facility-million-degree-seconds.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">原文</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">或者</span><a href="http://english.ipp.cas.cn/syxw/202112/t20211231_295485.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">相关报道</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-01-chinese-tokamak-facility-million-degree-seconds.html" target="_blank">Chinese tokamak facility achieves 120-million-degree <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">℃</span> for 1,056 seconds </a></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">by Bob Yirka<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; color: #665F54; border: none; white-space: normal; background-color: #FFFFFF; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-01-chinese-tokamak-facility-million-degree-seconds.html" target="_blank" style="overflow-wrap: break-word; color: rgb(102, 95, 84); border: none; white-space: normal; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Phys.org</a>，</span></span>Jan 05, 2022.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Researchers working at China's tokamak facility have announced that the team was able to hold 120-million-degree Celsius plasma for 1,056 seconds. In their announcement to the press, they noted that their achievement was a new record for holding superheated plasma.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The work is dedicated to the development of fusion as a power source that could replace coal-fired power plants and likely other renewable resource plants as well. The basic idea is to mimic the sun, which produces light and heat through fusion. Vibrating atoms become so excited when heated to high temperatures that their nuclei fuse together. In the sun, the atoms involved are hydrogen. When they fuse together, they form helium-4, an element that is lighter than the original four protons. The difference in mass is emitted as thermal energy, which is released from the sun as light and heat. Scientists have been working for many years to replicate the process in facilities like tokamak. In such facilities, small numbers of atoms are heated to very high temperatures using microwaves, creating plasma, and are kept from burning the donut-shaped facility containing them by holding them in place with magnets and lining the perimeter around them with materials that can sustain very high temperatures, such as carbon.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">In the tokamak, tritium and deuterium atoms are heated to very high temperatures—up to the point where they start fusing—approximately 150 million degrees Celsius. But doing so is just one step of the process. For a facility to produce energy on a continuous basis, fusion reactions must be self-sustaining, as is the case with the sun. So teams like those at tokamak facilities have been working to produce increasingly long reaction scenarios.</span><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"> </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">In their announcement, the team noted that their results show they are steadily moving toward their goals. Early last year, they held a sample of plasma at 120 million degrees Celsius for 101 seconds. They also state that their reactor has been designed to copy the fusion process in the sun as closely as possible; they refer to their facility as an "artificial sun."</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-12-fusion-energy-team-plasma-turbulence.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Toward fusion energy, team models plasma turbulence on the nation’s fastest supercomputer</span></a></p><p><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">More information: </span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="http://english.ipp.cas.cn/syxw/202112/t20211231_295485.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">1,056 Seconds, another world record for EAST</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">count<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">：</span> [2021-12-31]</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">1,056 seconds, a new pulse length world record for high temperature tokamak plasma has been achieved on the "artificial sun" in Institute of Plasma Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences (ASIPP). On December 30, the experimental advanced superconducting tokamak (EAST) set this milestone of tokamak operation for longer than 1,000 seconds by the end of 2021.　　</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Over the past 15 years of operation, EAST has achieved plasma operation with 1 megampere, 160 million degrees and 1,056 seconds separately. These milestones laying a solid scientific and experimental foundation toward the pursuing of the fusion energy.</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“ASIPP has a perfect team. We will face up to difficulties no matter how hard it is!”said the Prof. Yuntao SONG, Director-General of ASIPP.
