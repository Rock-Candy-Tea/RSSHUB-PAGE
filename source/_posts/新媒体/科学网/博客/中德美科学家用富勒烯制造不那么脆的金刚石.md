font-family: "times new roman";">http://dx.doi.org/10.1038/s41586-021-04122-w</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">.<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在该论文中，研究小组描述了他们制造准晶金刚石（</span>paracrystalline diamond<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的方法及其可能的用途。参与此项研究的有来自中国高压科技前沿研究中心（</span>Center for High Pressure Science and Technology Advanced Research, Beijing, China<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）、厦门大学材料学院（</span>College of Materials, Xiamen University, Xiamen, China<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）、燕山大学环境与化工学院（</span>College of Environmental and Chemical Engineering, Yanshan University, Qinhuangdao, China<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）德国拜罗伊特大学（</span>University of Bayreuth, Germany<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）以及美国乔治·梅森大学（</span>George Mason University, Fairfax, VA, USA<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的研究人员。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">先前的研究表明，金刚石是已知的最坚硬的材料，但它也很脆，尽管它们很硬，金刚石很容易被切割甚至破碎，这是因为它们有序的原子结构所致。多年来，科学家们一直试图合成出既保持硬度又不那么脆的金刚石。该团队的研究结果现在已经接近实现这一目标。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">目前，制造金刚石（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/diamonds/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">diamonds</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的方法是将一种碳基材料置于一个类似牢固的装置中，将其加热到非常高的温度，同时需要高压。在这项新的研究中，研究人员用同样的方法创造了一种秩序不那么有序的金刚石，但增加了一种新的扭曲，碳基材料是一批富勒烯（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-fragile-diamond-fullerenes.html" target="_self">fullerenes</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），也被称为巴克球</span>(<a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-fragile-diamond-fullerenes.html" target="_self">buckyballs</a>)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，即碳原子排列成的空心球状体。他们将这种材料加热到</span>900-1300 <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">℃，压力为</span>27-30<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">吉帕（</span>gigapascals<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>GPa<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。值得注意的是，施加的压力远低于用于制造商业金刚石的压力。在加工过程中，这些球体被迫坍塌，并形成透明的准晶金刚石（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-fragile-diamond-fullerenes.html" target="_self">transparent paracrystalline diamonds</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），可以在室温下提取。上述图</span>2<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">左边的是单晶金刚石</span>(<a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-fragile-diamond-fullerenes.html" target="_self">crystalline diamond</a>)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和右边的次晶金刚石</span>(<a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-fragile-diamond-fullerenes.html" target="_self">paracrystalline diamond</a>) <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的结构比较。在右边的以蓝绿色标记的立方碳原子单位，以黄色标记六角形碳原子单位，不规则的结构则用红色标出。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">在制造出顺序较差的金刚石后，研究人员在电子显微镜（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/electron+microscope/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">electron microscope</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）下观察它们，以进一步了解它们的结构。他们还对样品进行了</span>x<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">射线衍射和原子建模（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-fragile-diamond-fullerenes.html" target="_self">atomist modeling</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。在这样做的过程中，他们发现他们的金刚石正如他们所预期的那样，是由无序的</span><em>sp</em><sup>3</sup><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">杂化碳组成的。创造不那么脆的金刚石的目标已经实现。与最近另一项合成不那么脆的金刚石的研究结果不同，他们合成的金刚石不是完全无定形的</span>(<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这使其成为一种玻璃型的</span>)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，他们的结果是一种无定形金刚石的不完全晶体（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-11-fragile-diamond-fullerenes.html" target="_self">amorphous diamond paracrystal</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。这意味着它有一个中等距离的顺序，它的原子在短距离上是有序的（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/short+distances/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">short distances</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">），而不是在长距离上。因此，不存在原子平面，这意味着此种金刚石不能像天然钻石那样被切割。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1038/s41586-021-04122-w" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></strong></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Solids in nature can be generally classified into crystalline and non-crystalline states<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04122-w#ref-CR1" title="Zallen, R. The differentiation of the two states, however, could face fundamental challenges if the degree of long-range order in crystals is significantly reduced. Here we report a paracrystalline state of diamond that is distinct from either crystalline or amorphous diamond<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04122-w#ref-CR8" title="Hirai, H., Kondo, K., Yoshizawa, N. & Shiraishi, M. The paracrystalline diamond reported in this work, consisting of sub-nanometre-sized paracrystallites that possess a well-defined crystalline medium-range order up to a few atomic shells<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04122-w#ref-CR4" title="Elliott, S. Nature 439, 419–425 (2006)." target="_blank">5</a>,<a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04122-w#ref-CR11" title="Treacy, M. M. J. & Borisenko, K. B. The structural characteristics of the paracrystalline diamond were identified through a combination of X-ray diffraction, high-resolution transmission microscopy and advanced molecular dynamics simulation. The formation of paracrystalline diamond is a result of densely distributed nucleation sites developed in compressed C<sub>60</sub> as well as pronounced second-nearest-neighbour short-range order in amorphous diamond due to strong <em>sp</em><sup>3</sup> bonding. The discovery of paracrystalline diamond adds an unusual diamond form to the enriched carbon family<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-04122-w#ref-CR14" title="Oganov, A.
