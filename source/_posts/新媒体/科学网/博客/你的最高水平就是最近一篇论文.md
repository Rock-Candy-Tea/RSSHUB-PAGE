<p style="overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: center; margin-bottom: 25px; margin-top: 25px; line-height: 2em;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; line-height: 20.7px; letter-spacing: 1px; font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; font-size: 18px;">“你的最高水平就是最近一篇论文”<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; line-height: 18.4px; font-size: 16px;">--- 国际学术界流行语</span></span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 15px; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; font-size: 14px;"><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; line-height: 200%;">    这句流行语说的是学者应该努力的方向，并不是说这是客观事实。一项研究成果的质量，是由众多因素综合决定的，包括天时地利人和以及作者的努力与能力等等。实际上，</span><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; line-height: 200%; background: white;">那些最具开创性的研究成果，往往是在作者年轻时期做出来的。</span><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; line-height: 200%;">质量是一个点上的突破，不是面上的扩充。突破一个点，需要的是人类最珍贵的创造力，而通常来讲一个人的创造力在年轻时期是最旺盛的。</span></span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 15px; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; font-size: 14px;"><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; line-height: 200%;">    那么，对于一个学者来说，如何在一生的科研之路上努力突破自己、做出更高质量的研究成果呢？我觉得，跨学科研究是突破自己的可行之路。</span></span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 15px; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; font-size: 14px;"><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; line-height: 200%;">    跨学科研究不是简单地和其它领域的学者合作，因为现在学科越来越细，两个学科的人说的是不同的语言。突破性的跨学科研究，是一个领域的顶级学者，放下架子，跑到另外一个领域，从博士开始学起，学到另外那个领域的最高点，可以进行创造。也就是说，一个脑子，同时是两个领域的顶级专家，最尖端的思想在一个脑子里碰撞，这样才最有可能做出真正跨学科的突破性成果。</span></span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 15px; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; font-size: 14px;"><span style="font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; line-height: 200%;">    这也是我自己三十多年来所走的科研之路。下面是我的主要研究成果的简要介绍，从我的座右铭“好好学习，天天向上”开始。</span></span></p><p style="overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: center; line-height: 2em; margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 5px;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; line-height: 20.7px; letter-spacing: 1px; font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; font-size: 18px;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; line-height: 18.4px; font-size: 16px;"></span></span><br></p><p style="margin-bottom: 25px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: center; line-height: 2em; margin-top: 5px;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-family: 微软雅黑, "Microsoft YaHei"; font-size: 18px;">“好好学习，天天向上”</span></p><p style="margin-top: 1.12em; margin-bottom: 15px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; line-height: 16.1px; text-align: center;"><strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 19px; line-height: 21.85px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">RESEARCH CONTRIBUTIONS: AN INTRODUCTION</span></strong></p><p style="margin-top: 1.12em; margin-bottom: 16px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">For more than twenty years, Li-Xin Wang’s works on fuzzy systems and fuzzy control have been and continue to be very influential. His works are characterized by originality, rigor and easy-to-follow. He initiated four very active research areas in fuzzy systems during the last two decades: adaptive fuzzy control, universal approximation of fuzzy systems, design of fuzzy systems from data, and universal hierarchical fuzzy models, which are briefly summarized as follows.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 1.12em; margin-bottom: 8px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">Although Zadeh proposed fuzzy sets and fuzzy logic as early as 1965, till the early 1990’s fuzzy systems and control were largely rule based expert systems and heuristic control which had limited modelling power, lacking of learning and adaptive capabilities, and unable to use widely available data. The fundamental changes and breakthroughs in this field happened around the early 1990’s, which have led to model based fuzzy systems and control today and Li-Xin Wang is one of the most important researchers who led these fundamental changes and breakthroughs:</span></p><p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 8px; margin-left: 19px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Symbol;">·<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-variant-numeric: normal; font-variant-east-asian: normal; font-stretch: normal; font-size: 9px; line-height: normal; font-family: "Times New Roman";">      </span></span><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">He is the first researcher proving that fuzzy systems are universal approximators (his two papers for these results – References 4 and 6 in the Record of Accomplishments section below – have been cited <strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">4531</strong> times; Note: All numbers of citations are from Google Scholar as of June 2021), which established the theoretical foundation that fuzzy systems are general nonlinear system models and representations and therefore are widely applicable. This fundamental result has led and created an active research area in fuzzy systems during the last twenty years – fuzzy approximation of general nonlinear systems. </span></p><p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 8px; margin-left: 19px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Symbol;">·<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-variant-numeric: normal; font-variant-east-asian: normal; font-stretch: normal; font-size: 9px; line-height: normal; font-family: "Times New Roman";">      </span></span><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">He is one of the first researchers proposing learning or generating fuzzy systems from data (his three papers for these results – References 3, 7 and 11 below – have been cited <strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">4599</strong> times) and this has resulted in and formed the research area – learning and identification of fuzzy systems from data, one of the most important and most active research areas in fuzzy systems during the last two decades. Specifically, the paper Reference 7 below published in the first FUZZ-IEEE conference was one of the three groups showing for the first time how to use the back-propagation algorithm to train a fuzzy system, and the paper Reference 3 below proposed the so-called Wang-Mendel method – the first published method for extracting fuzzy IF-THEN rules from data, which is now the standard against which all other methods are compared.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 8px; margin-left: 19px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Symbol;">·<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-variant-numeric: normal; font-variant-east-asian: normal; font-stretch: normal; font-size: 9px; line-height: normal; font-family: "Times New Roman";">      </span></span><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">He is the first researcher establishing the basic mathematical framework for adaptive fuzzy systems and control and proving the most fundamental stability and convergence properties. His book published in 1994 on this topic – Reference 1 below – is the most cited book in this area and has been cited <strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">5110</strong> times. His 1993-1996 papers on the subject – References 5, 8 and 10 below – have been cited <strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">2700</strong> times. This book and these papers basically have led and created another most important and active research area in fuzzy systems during the last two decades – adaptive fuzzy systems and control, which has pushed fuzzy control forward from the early stage rule-based expert systems and heuristic control to the more powerful model based fuzzy control for general nonlinear systems which can be analyzed and designed in a mathematically rigorous fashion.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 8px; margin-left: 19px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Symbol;">·<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-variant-numeric: normal; font-variant-east-asian: normal; font-stretch: normal; font-size: 9px; line-height: normal; font-family: "Times New Roman";">      </span></span><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">He is the first researcher proving that hierarchical fuzzy systems are universal approximators and performing detailed analysis and design of hierarchical fuzzy systems from data (References 9 and 12 below, cited <strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">582</strong> times). Hierarchical fuzzy systems are more powerful modelling structures than standard fuzzy systems, and extending standard fuzzy systems to hierarchical fuzzy systems is similar to the extension of single-hidden-layer neural networks to deep neural networks. With the extraordinary success of deep neural networks, we believe that hierarchical fuzzy systems could also dramatically improve the performance of the fuzzy system approaches to a wide variety of practical problems.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 1.12em; margin-bottom: 8px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">In the educational frontier, his textbook “A Course in Fuzzy Systems and Control” – Reference 2 below – is one of the most widely-used textbooks in the field and has been cited <strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">4938</strong> times.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 1.12em; margin-bottom: 8px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><strong><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">In recent years</span></strong><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">, Li-Xin Wang continues his pioneering style of research to explore new frontiers – in finance, economics, and social sciences – for the fuzzy field. Specifically:</span></p><p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 16px; margin-left: 19px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Symbol;">·<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-variant-numeric: normal; font-variant-east-asian: normal; font-stretch: normal; font-size: 9px; line-height: normal; font-family: "Times New Roman";">      </span></span><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">He creatively used fuzzy systems theory to convert the technical trading heuristics commonly used by stock practitioners into price dynamical equations, studied a number of key problems in financial economics – equilibrium, volatility, predictability and independency – from a new angle, and developed some practically very successful trading strategies based on the fuzzy models (L.X. Wang, “Dynamical models of stock prices based on technical trading rules Part I: The models; Part II: Analysis of the model; Part III: Application to Hong Kong stocks,” <em style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">IEEE Trans. on Fuzzy Systems </em>23(4): 787-801 (Part I); 23(4): 1127-1141 (Part II); 23(5): 1680-1697 (Part III), 2015). Specifically, rather than following the traditional approach of concocting stochastic models of market behaviour, his new models are constructive, in the sense that they can be directly incorporated into the design of financial engineering systems, leading to scrutable implementations whose performance can be analyzed in a causal fashion. These results demonstrate to a wider audience the usefulness of fuzzy theory in the study of these very important practical problems.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 16px; margin-left: 19px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Symbol;">·<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-variant-numeric: normal; font-variant-east-asian: normal; font-stretch: normal; font-size: 9px; line-height: normal; font-family: "Times New Roman";">      </span></span><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">He initiated Fuzzy Opinion Networks – a new mathematical framework for the evolution and propagation of human opinions and their uncertainties across various types of social network structures (L.X. Wang and J.M. Mendel, “Fuzzy opinion networks: A mathematical framework for the evolution of opinions and their uncertainties across social networks,” <em style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">IEEE Trans. on Fuzzy Systems</em>, Vol. 24, No. 4, pp. 880-905, 2016). He used this new mathematical model to study: (1) the interaction between investor social networks and stock price dynamics (L.X. Wang, “Modeling stock price dynamics with fuzzy opinion networks,” <em style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">IEEE Trans. on Fuzzy Systems</em>, Vol. 25, No. 2, pp. 277-301, 2017), and (2) the top-down social organizations and the bottom-up election scenarios (L.X. Wang, “Hierarchical fuzzy opinion networks: Top-down for social organizations and bottom-up for election,” <em style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">IEEE Trans. on Fuzzy Systems</em>, Vol. 28, No. 7, pp. 1265-1275, 2020). This research opened a new door for fuzzy researchers to play an important role in the exciting interdisciplinary fields – social networks and opinion dynamics.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 16px; margin-left: 19px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Symbol;">·<span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-variant-numeric: normal; font-variant-east-asian: normal; font-stretch: normal; font-size: 9px; line-height: normal; font-family: "Times New Roman";">      </span></span><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">He proposed Deep Interpretable Fuzzy Models and fast training algorithms for the new models (L.X. Wang, “Fast training algorithms for deep convolutional fuzzy systems with application to stock index prediction,” <em style="overflow-wrap: break-word;">IEEE Trans. on Fuzzy Systems</em>, Vol. 28, No. 7, pp. 1301-1314, 2020), which overcome the computational and interpretability problems of the popular black-box and iteratively trained deep neural networks.</span></p><p style="margin-top: 16px; margin-bottom: 8px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">Record of Accomplishments</span></strong></p><p style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 16px; overflow-wrap: break-word; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;"><strong style="overflow-wrap: break-word;"><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;">Li-Xin Wang</span></strong><span style="overflow-wrap: break-word; font-size: 15px; font-family: Calibri, sans-serif;"> received the Ph.D. degree from the Department of Electrical Engineering, University of Southern California, Los Angeles, CA, USA, in 1992. From 1992 to 1993, he was a Postdoctoral Fellow with the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, University of California at Berkeley, supported by the research grant of Professor Zadeh. From 1993 to 2007, he was on the faculty of the Department of Electronic and Computer Engineering, The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST). In 2007, he resigned from his tenured position at HKUST to become an independent researcher and investor in the stock and real estate markets in China.
