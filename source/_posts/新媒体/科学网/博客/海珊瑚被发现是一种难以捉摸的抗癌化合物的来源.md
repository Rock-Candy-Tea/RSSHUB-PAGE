font-family: 宋体;">与注入猎物的有毒化学物质不同，珊瑚使用它们的化学物质来抵御试图吃掉它们的捕食者。由于它们是用来食用的，因此软珊瑚化学物质很容易消化。同样，从这些类型的化合物中提取的药物</span>,<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">应该能够通过一杯水作为药丸服用，而不是通过注射或其他更具侵入性的方式服用。“这些化合物更难找到，但它们更容易在实验室中制造，也更容易作为药物服用，”埃里克·施密特说。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">几十年来，这些可能性都是遥不可及的。达到这一点不仅需要正确的专业知识，而且也与运气有关。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">寻找源头（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/sea-corals-found-to-be-a-source-of-an-elusive-anti-cancer-compound/" target="_blank">Hunting for the Source</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">保罗·塞萨在美国佛罗里达海岸附近的一种常见软珊瑚中发现了这个备受追捧的化合物——距离他兄弟的公寓只有一英里。在</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">世纪</span>90<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年代，海洋科学家报告说，澳大利亚附近的一种稀有珊瑚携带一种化学物质，即五加素（</span>eleutherobin<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），具有抗癌特性。这种化学物质破坏了细胞骨架，这是细胞中的一个关键支架，软珊瑚用它来防御捕食者。但实验室研究表明，该化合物也是癌细胞生长的有效抑制剂。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在之后的几十年里，科学家们搜索但无法找到药物开发所需数量的传说中的“圣杯”化学物质（</span>“holy grail” chemical<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），并且在不了解化学物质是如何制造的情况下无法解决问题。教条认为，与其他种类的海洋生物类似，这种化学物质是由生活在动物体内的共生生物（</span>symbiotic organisms<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）合成的。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">保罗·塞萨说：“这没有任何意义，我们知道珊瑚必须制造五加素。”</span> <span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">虽然如此，他和埃里克·施密特推断，一些软珊瑚物种没有共生生物，但它们的身体却含有相同类别的化学物质。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">保罗·塞萨潜水寻找软珊瑚（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/sea-corals-found-to-be-a-source-of-an-elusive-anti-cancer-compound/" target="_blank">Paul Scesa dives for soft corals</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">保罗·塞萨博士在佛罗里达海岸潜水寻找软珊瑚（</span>Fig. 4<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。他研究软珊瑚化学物质作为药物先导的潜力。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">解开谜团似乎是保罗·塞萨的一项工作。作为一个在佛罗里达长大的男孩，海洋是他的游乐场，他花了无数小时探索海洋深处和野生动物。在研究生院，他对有机化学产生了浓厚的兴趣，并将这两种兴趣结合起来，以更好地了解海洋的化学多样性。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">后来，他加入了天然产物科学家埃里克·施密特的实验室，任务是追查先导药物（</span>drug lead<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的来源。保罗·塞萨怀疑他熟悉的珊瑚物种可能找到了答案，于是将小型活体样本从佛罗里达州带到了犹他州，真正的追踪从此开始了。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">解码配方（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/sea-corals-found-to-be-a-source-of-an-elusive-anti-cancer-compound/" target="_blank">Decoding the Recipe</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">下一步是查明珊瑚的遗传密码是否带有制造这种化合物的指令。</span>DNA<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">技术的进步最近使快速拼凑任何物种的密码成为可能。困难在于科学家们不知道制造这种化学物质的指令应该是什么样子的。想象一下在一本烹饪大全中搜索某个食谱，只是你不知道书中的任何单词是什么意思。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">埃里克·施密特和保罗·塞萨（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/sea-corals-found-to-be-a-source-of-an-elusive-anti-cancer-compound/" target="_blank">Eric Schmidt and Paul Scesa</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">埃里克·施密特博士和保罗·塞萨博士正在通过这些步骤来制造潜在的抗癌化合物五加素（</span><a href="https://media.iupac.org/publications/pac/2007/pdf/7902x0173.pdf" target="_blank">eleutherobin</a>, Fig. 5<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。</span></span></p><p style="text-align:center"><a href="https://media.iupac.org/publications/pac/2007/pdf/7902x0173.pdf" target="_blank"><img src="https://cors.zfour.workers.dev/?http://image.sciencenet.cn/home/202207/02/153602up70hifqi2i5aa5i.jpg" title alt="360截图20220702144633406.jpg" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"></a><br></p><p style="text-align: left; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;"> </span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“这就像走进黑暗，在你不知道问题的地方寻找答案，”埃里克·施密特评论道。</span><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">他们找到了珊瑚</span>DNA<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">中与其他物种类似化合物的遗传指令相似的区域，从而解决了这个问题。在对实验室中生长的细菌进行编程，使其遵循珊瑚</span>DNA<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">特定于软珊瑚的指令后，这些微生物能够复制制造潜在癌症治疗剂的第一步。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">这证明软珊瑚是五加素的来源。它还证明了应该可以在实验室中制造这种化合物。他们现在的工作重点是填补化合物配方中缺失的步骤，并确定生产大量潜在药物的最佳方法。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">生物反应器的应用（</span></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;"></span></strong><strong><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/sea-corals-found-to-be-a-source-of-an-elusive-anti-cancer-compound/" target="_blank">Using a bioreactor to produce the compound</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">保罗·塞萨使用生物反应器生产大量的化学物质，这些化学物质被发现在自然界中存在量很少（</span>Fig. 6<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">保罗·塞萨说：“我希望有朝一日能把这些交给医生。我认为它正在从海底到实验室再到临床应用的途中。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"> (</span><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41589-022-01027-1" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Diterpenes are major defensive small molecules that enable soft corals to survive without a tough exterior skeleton, and, until now, their biosynthetic origin has remained intractable. Furthermore, biomedical application of these molecules has been hampered by lack of supply. Here, we identify and characterize coral-encoded terpene cyclase genes that produce the eunicellane precursor of eleutherobin and cembrene, representative precursors for the >2,500 terpenes found in octocorals. Related genes are found in all sequenced octocorals and form their own clade, indicating a potential ancient origin concomitant with the split between the hard and soft corals. Eleutherobin biosynthetic genes are colocalized in a single chromosomal region. This demonstrates that, like plants and microbes, animals also harbor defensive biosynthetic gene clusters, supporting a recombinational model to explain why specialized or defensive metabolites are adjacently encoded in the genome.</span></p><p style="text-align:center"><img src="https://cors.zfour.workers.dev/?http://image.sciencenet.cn/home/202207/02/154110txav0rngme0senpn.jpg" title alt="41589_2022_1027_Figa_HTML.webp.jpg" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s41589-022-01026-2" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";"> </span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">(DOI: </span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s41589-022-01026-2" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">10.1038/s41589-022-01026-2</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">)</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Octocorals are major contributors of terpenoid chemical diversity in the ocean. Natural products from other sessile marine animals are primarily biosynthesized by symbiotic microbes rather than by the host. Here, we challenge this long-standing paradigm by describing a monophyletic lineage of animal-encoded terpene cyclases (TCs) ubiquitous in octocorals. We characterized 15 TC enzymes from nine genera, several of which produce precursors of iconic coral-specific terpenoids, such as pseudopterosin, lophotoxin and eleutherobin. X-ray crystallography revealed that coral TCs share conserved active site residues and structural features with bacterial TCs. The identification of coral TCs enabled the targeted identification of the enzyme that constructs the coral-exclusive capnellane scaffold. Several TC genes are colocalized with genes that encode enzymes known to modify terpenes. This work presents an example of biosynthetic capacity in the kingdom Animalia that rivals the chemical complexity generated by plants, unlocking the biotechnological potential of octocorals for biomedical applications.</span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://cors.zfour.workers.dev/?http://image.sciencenet.cn/home/202207/02/154013fi8iki5sz9hdbniz.jpg" title alt="41589_2022_1026_Figa_HTML.webp.jpg" style="text-align: center; white-space: normal;" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"><br></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
