font-size: 18px;">Wnt</span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的拮抗因子在牙齿发育中可能更安全，因此他们考虑了该基因</span><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;">USAG-1</span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。</span><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;">Katsu Takahashi</span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">补充说：</span><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;">“</span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">我们知道抑制</span><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;">USAG-1</span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">有益于牙齿生长。我们不知道这是否足够。</span><span style="font-family: "times new roman"; font-size: 18px;">”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">因此，科学家研究了几种针对</span>USAG-1<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的单克隆抗体的作用。单克隆抗体通常用于治疗癌症、关节炎和疫苗开发。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">USAG-1<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">与</span>BMP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和</span>Wnt<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">都相互作用。结果，几种抗体导致小鼠的出生和存活率低下，从而证实了</span>BMP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和</span>Wnt<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">用这种抗体进行的实验表明，</span>BMP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">信号传导对于确定小鼠的牙齿数量至关重要。而且，单次施用足以产生整颗牙齿。随后的实验表明，在雪貂中有相同的好处。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Katsu Takahashi<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">解释说：</span>“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">白鼬是一种双齿动物，具有与人类相似的牙齿模式。我们的下一个计划是测试对其他动物（如猪和狗）的抗体。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">该研究是第一个显示单克隆抗体对牙齿再生有益的研究，并为目前只能通过植入物和其他人工措施解决的临床问题提供了新的治疗框架。该研究的另一位作者，福井大学的</span>Manabu Sugai<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a><br></p><p style="box-sizing: inherit; margin-top: 3.75px; margin-bottom: 0px; width: 669px; transition: background-color 1s ease-in-out 1s; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: Roboto, "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; white-space: normal; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><em style="box-sizing: inherit;">Uterine sensitization–associated gene-1</em> (<em style="box-sizing: inherit;">USAG-1</em>) deficiency leads to enhanced bone morphogenetic protein (BMP) signaling, leading to supernumerary teeth formation. Furthermore, antibodies interfering with binding of USAG-1 to BMP, but not lipoprotein receptor–related protein 5/6 (LRP5/6), accelerate tooth development. Since USAG-1 inhibits Wnt and BMP signals, the essential factors for tooth development, via direct binding to BMP and Wnt coreceptor LRP5/6, we hypothesized that USAG-1 plays key regulatory roles in suppressing tooth development. However, the involvement of USAG-1 in various types of congenital tooth agenesis remains unknown. Here, we show that blocking USAG-1 function through <em style="box-sizing: inherit;">USAG-1</em> knockout or anti–USAG-1 antibody administration relieves congenital tooth agenesis caused by various genetic abnormalities in mice. Our results demonstrate that <em style="box-sizing: inherit;">USAG-1</em> controls the number of teeth by inhibiting development of potential tooth germs in wild-type or mutant mice missing teeth. Anti–USAG-1 antibody administration is, therefore, a promising approach for tooth regeneration therapy.</span></p>                    <br><br>
