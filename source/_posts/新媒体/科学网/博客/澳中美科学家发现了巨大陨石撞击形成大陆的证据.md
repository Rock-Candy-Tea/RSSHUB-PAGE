font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">澳中美研究人员合作完成的新研究，提供了迄今为止最有力的证据，表明地球大陆是由巨大的陨石撞击形成的，这种撞击在地球</span>45<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">亿年历史的头</span>10<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“通过检查西澳大利亚皮尔巴拉克拉通</span>(</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-evidence-giant-meteorite-impacts-continents.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Pilbara Craton in Western Australia</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">岩石中锆石（</span>zircon<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）矿物的微小晶体，我们发现了这些巨大陨石撞击的证据，它们代表着地球上保存最完好的古地壳（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-evidence-giant-meteorite-impacts-continents.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">ancient crust</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）遗迹，”蒂姆·约翰逊说。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“对这些锆石晶体中氧同位素组成的研究，揭示了一个‘自上而下’的过程（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-evidence-giant-meteorite-impacts-continents.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">'top-down' process</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">），从地表附近的岩石融化开始，然后进一步深入，这与巨型陨石撞击的地质效应（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-08-evidence-giant-meteorite-impacts-continents.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">geological effect</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）相一致。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">我们的研究提供了第一个确凿的证据，表明最终形成大陆的过程始于巨大的陨石撞击，与导致恐龙灭绝的过程类似，但发生在数十亿年前。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">蒂姆·约翰逊博士说，了解地球大陆的形成和持续演化是至关重要的，因为地球上大部分的生物、所有的人类和几乎所有重要的矿藏都在这些大陆上。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“重要的是，这些大陆上蕴藏着锂（</span>lithium<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，</span>Li<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）、锡</span>(tin, Sn)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和镍</span>(nickel, Ni)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">等关键金属，这些大宗商品对于新兴的绿色技术</span>(</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/green+technologies/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">green technologies</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">至关重要，需要它们来履行我们减轻气候变化</span>( </span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/climate+change/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">climate change</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的义务，”蒂姆·约翰逊说。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">“<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这些矿藏</span>(</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/mineral+deposits/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">是地壳分化过程的最终结果，这一过程始于最早的大陆块的形成，皮尔巴拉克拉通只是众多大陆块中的一个。与地球上其他地区的古大陆地壳相关的数据</span>,<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">似乎显示出了与西澳大利亚州相似的模式。我们想测试我们在这些古老岩石上的发现，看看我们的模型是否如我们所怀疑的那样更广泛地适用。</span>”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这项工作得到了西澳大利亚政府勘探奖励计划</span>(</span><a href="http://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-04956-y" style="text-decoration: underline; Here using the oxygen isotope compositions of dated magmatic zircon, we show that the Pilbara Craton in Western Australia, Earth’s best-preserved Archaean (4.0–2.5 billion years ago (Ga)) continental remnant, was built in three stages. Stage 1 zircons (3.6–3.4 Ga) form two age clusters with one-third recording submantle δ<sup>18</sup>O, indicating crystallization from evolved magmas derived from hydrothermally altered basaltic crust like that in modern-day Iceland<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-04956-y#ref-CR1" title="Bindeman, I. et al. Shallow melting is consistent with giant impacts that typified the first billion years of Earth history<sup><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-022-04956-y#ref-CR3" title="Johnson, T.
