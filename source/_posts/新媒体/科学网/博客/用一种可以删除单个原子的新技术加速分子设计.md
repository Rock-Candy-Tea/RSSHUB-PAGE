font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">Science</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）杂志网站发表——</span><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abo4282#con1" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">Jisoo Woo</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">, </span><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abo4282#con2" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">Alec H. Christian</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">, </span><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abo4282#con3" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">Mark D. Levin</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">. Scaffold hopping by net photochemical carbon deletion of azaarenes. </span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abo4282" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">Science</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">, 28 Apr 2022, 376(6592): 527-532. </span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abo4282" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank">DOI: 10.1126/science.abo4282</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">芝加哥大学的化学家们希望这一突破能够帮助加速药物的发现。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">每次一种新的抗癌药物被宣布出来，都代表着数百名科学家花费数年时间在幕后工作，设计和测试一种新的分子。因为药物不仅要有效，而且要尽可能安全，易于制造，这些研究人员必须在数千种可能的化学结构选择中做出选择。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">但是，即使研究人员只是想改变一个碳原子，为测试构建每一个可能的分子结构也是一个费力的过程。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">芝加哥大学化学家和默克制药公司（</span>Merck & Co.<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）在《科学》（</span></span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abo4282" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Science</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）杂志上公布的一项新技术，提供了一种跨越这一过程的方法，允许科学家快速、轻松地产生感兴趣的新分子。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">这项新研究的合著者、芝加哥大学化学助理教授马克·莱文</span>(Mark Levin)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">说，“这让你可以对一个复杂的分子进行微调，而不必重新开始整个设计过程。我们希望通过减少这个过程中的时间和精力来加速发现。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">推倒房子重建（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/speeding-up-molecule-design-with-a-new-technique-that-can-delete-single-atoms/" target="_self">Bulldozing the house</a></strong><strong><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）</span></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">当研究人员考虑一种分子时，他们可能想要测试许多调整。例如，将一对氢原子而不是氮原子连接在一起，可能会使身体更容易吸收药物。也许去掉一个碳原子就能减少某种副作用。但实际上，制造这种新分子会出乎意料地困难。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">马克·莱文说：“尽管它表面上看起来像一个小开关，但如果不从头开始，有些事情是无法解决的。这就好像你在和一个承包商说要重新装修你房子里的一个浴室，他说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">‘对不起，我们必须把整个房子推倒重新再建。’”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">马克·莱文实验室的目标是避开这个繁琐的过程，允许科学家对一个几乎完成的分子进行一到两个修改。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在这个例子中，他们希望能够从一类被称为喹啉氧化物（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/speeding-up-molecule-design-with-a-new-technique-that-can-delete-single-atoms/" target="_self">quinoline oxides</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的分子中剪下一个单键，并将其转化为另一种被称为吲哚类（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/speeding-up-molecule-design-with-a-new-technique-that-can-delete-single-atoms/" target="_self">indoles</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的分子。马克·莱文说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“从本质上讲，我们想要去掉一个碳原子，而让其他所有原子仍然连接在一起，就像它从未存在过一样。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">他们无意中发现了一种</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">世纪五六十年代的老技术，利用光催化某些反应。它之所以没有被广泛使用，是因为该方法虽然强大，但不加区分。</span>20<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">世纪</span>60<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年代使用的水银灯发出全光谱的光，这引发了分子中太多的反应，而不仅仅是科学家们想要的反应。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">但是，芝加哥大学博士生、这篇新论文的第一作者</span>Jisoo Woo<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">认为，在过去十年中出现的较新的</span>LED<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">灯，结果可能会有所不同。这些灯可以被设定为只发出特定波长的光。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">它是可行的。如果只发出特定波长的光，科学家们就只能催化一种特定的反应，这种反应可以快速而容易地切断碳键。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">马克·莱文、</span>Jisoo Woo<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">和他们的同事想知道这种技术的应用范围有多大。他们与默克制药公司的科学家亚历克·克里斯蒂安</span>(Alec Christian)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">合作，在几组不同的分子上进行了测试。这项技术在几个分子家族中都显示出了希望。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Jisoo Woo<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“例如，我们证明我们可以将降胆固醇药物匹伐他汀（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/speeding-up-molecule-design-with-a-new-technique-that-can-delete-single-atoms/" target="_self">pitavastatin</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）转化为另一种降胆固醇药物氟伐他汀（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/speeding-up-molecule-design-with-a-new-technique-that-can-delete-single-atoms/" target="_self">fluvastatin</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。这是两种完全不同的分子，只因为一个碳原子的缺失而相互关联。在使用这种方法之前，你必须用两种完全不同的工艺和原料来制作。但是我们能够通过一次转化把一种药物变成另一种药物。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">科学家们希望这一过程能够简化和加快设计新分子的过程，尤其是那些涉及这种特殊转变的分子，化学家们称之为“支架跳跃（</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/speeding-up-molecule-design-with-a-new-technique-that-can-delete-single-atoms/" target="_self">scaffold hop</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）”。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">马克·莱文说：“有各种各样的支架跳跃，可以产生非常有用的分子，但所涉及的时间太长了，所以化学家们从来没有考虑过。因为有许多团队没有时间重新开始，可能会有惊人的药物化合物隐藏在那里。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">亚历克·克里斯蒂安表示</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“我曾经看到过一些项目走到十字路口，因为有人想尝试这样的改变，但甚至需要一个月的时间来解决最初的化学反应。然而，通过这个过程，你可以在一天内得到答案。我想很多人会想要使用这种方法。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">为了进行部分研究，科学家们使用了位于美国能源部阿贡国家实验室（</span>U.S. Department of Energy’s Argonne National Laboratory<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的大型</span>x<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">射线同步加速器“先进光子源”</span>(Advanced Photon Source)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">中的</span>ChemMatCARS<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">光束（</span>ChemMatCARS beamline<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">此研究得到了帕卡德基金会（</span>Packard Foundation<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）、美国国立卫生研究院（</span>National Institutes of Health<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）、美国国家科学基金会（</span>National Science Foundation<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）以及美国能源部资助。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abo4282" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">原文</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">或者</span><a href="https://scitechdaily.com/speeding-up-molecule-design-with-a-new-technique-that-can-delete-single-atoms/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">相关报道</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abo4282" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Carbon excision</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Quinolines and indoles are both very common core motifs in drug molecules. Because they differ by just a single carbon in their ring frameworks, it would be useful to interconvert them during structure-activity relationship studies. However, aromatic stabilization in these compounds makes that process difficult. Woo <em style="box-sizing: border-box">et al</em>. now report that narrow-wavelength irradiation of quinoline <em style="box-sizing: border-box">N</em>-oxides in the near-ultraviolet range followed by acid treatment cleanly excises a ring carbon to produce an indole, avoiding secondary photoproducts that were previously observed using broadband light. —JSY</span></p><p></p><section><p style="line-height: 2em;"><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abo4282" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Discovery chemists routinely identify purpose-tailored molecules through an iterative structural optimization approach, but the preparation of each successive candidate in a compound series can rarely be conducted in a manner matching their thought process. This is because many of the necessary chemical transformations required to modify compound cores in a straightforward fashion are not applicable in complex contexts. We report a method that addresses one facet of this problem by allowing chemists to hop directly between chemically distinct heteroaromatic scaffolds. Specifically, we show that selective photolysis of quinoline <em style="box-sizing: border-box">N</em>-oxides with 390-nanometer light followed by acid-promoted rearrangement affords <em style="box-sizing: border-box">N</em>-acylindoles while showing broad compatibility with medicinally relevant functionality. Applications to late-stage skeletal modification of compounds of pharmaceutical interest and more complex transformations involving serial single-atom changes are demonstrated.</span></p></section><p></p>                    <br><br>
