font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">removing dangerous chemicals from drinking water</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）的高吸水性聚合物。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">研究人员设想，利用这种创新方法，将难以回收的塑料垃圾转化为高价值的聚合物。他们首先使用在运输过程中用来保护电子产品的泡沫塑料包装进行研究，因为这些产品最终会被填埋。消费后泡沫样品由北卡罗来纳州达勒姆（</span>Durham, N.C.<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）的回收公司（</span></span><a href="https://high3.org/" target="_blank" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">High Cube LLC</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">通过有选择性地从聚烯烃中提取氢原子，化学家们想出了一种方法，将这种一次性塑料延长为一种高价值的塑料，称为离聚物（</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2022-02-chemists-radical-easier-profitable-recycle.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-family: "times new roman";">）</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";"><strong><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abh4308" target="_blank">A clean break for C–H bonds</a></strong></span><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Carbon–hydrogen (C–H) bonds are ubiquitous in pharmaceuticals and plastics but are difficult to transform. Fazekas et al. report a versatile reagent that strips hydrogen without immediately trapping the carbon. Heating or photolysis of the reagent produces a pair of radicals, one of which rapidly cleaves a C–H bond while the other remains comparatively inert. A wide variety of other radical sources can then intercede to form carbon–halogen, carbon–carbon, and carbon–sulfur bonds. A two-step upcycling sequence that added imidazolium groups to postconsumer polyethylene foam produced a potentially valuable ionomer. —JSY</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";"><strong><a href="https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abh4308" target="_blank">Abstract</a></strong></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">The ability to selectively introduce diverse functionality onto hydrocarbons is of substantial value in the synthesis of both small molecules and polymers. This broad reaction platform enabled the functionalization of postconsumer polyolefins in infrastructure used to process plastic waste. Furthermore, the chemoselective placement of ionic functionality onto a branched polyolefin using carbon–hydrogen bond functionalization upcycled the material from a thermoplastic into a tough elastomer with the tensile properties of high-value polyolefin ionomers.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
