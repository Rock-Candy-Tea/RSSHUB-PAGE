font-family: 宋体;">简称</span>cAMP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）是重要的第二信使（</span>secondary messenger<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">），它控制铜绿假单胞菌的碳代谢，</span>IVa<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。当</span>cAMP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">调节细菌中的某种生理功能时，通常会同时调节影响该生理功能的多种成分蛋白的表达水平。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">在这项研究中，研究人员通过将光激活腺苷酸环化酶（</span>adenylate cyclase<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）基因</span>(bPAC)<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">引入细菌，开发出一种具有光依赖性胞内</span>cAMP<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">裸鼠皮下感染模型显示，</span><em>pactm</em><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">感染引起的皮肤损伤面积增加了</span>14<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">倍（见上述图示）。因此，本研究为构建可控感染实验模型提供了一种解决方案。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">此外，研究者通过宏观照明模式的设计指导了细菌种群的扩张方向，为微生物间接触依赖相互作用的研究提供了便利。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍仅供参考，更多信息敬请注意浏览原文或者</span><a href="https://phys.org/news/2021-05-strain-optically-bacteria-movement-behavior.html" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">相关报道</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">。</span></p><h2><a href="https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acssynbio.0c00559" target="_blank"><span style="font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a></h2><p style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none; line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) is an important secondary messenger that controls carbon metabolism, type IVa pili biogenesis, and virulence in <em style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none;">Pseudomonas aeruginosa</em>. Precise manipulation of bacterial intracellular cAMP levels may enable tunable control of twitching motility or virulence, and optogenetic tools are attractive because they afford excellent spatiotemporal resolution and are easy to operate. Here, we developed an engineered <em style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none;">P. aeruginosa</em> strain (termed <em style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none;">pactm</em>) with light-dependent intracellular cAMP levels through introducing a photoactivated adenylate cyclase gene (<em style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none;">bPAC</em>) into bacteria. On blue light illumination, <em style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none;">pactm</em> displayed a 15-fold increase in the expression of the cAMP responsive promoter and an 8-fold increase in its twitching activity. The skin lesion area of nude mouse in a subcutaneous infection model after 2-day <em style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none;">pactm</em> inoculation was increased 14-fold by blue light, making <em style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none;">pactm</em> suitable for applications in controllable bacterial host infection. In addition, we achieved directional twitching motility of <em style="box-sizing: border-box; outline: none;">pactm</em> colonies through localized light illumination, which will facilitate the studies of contact-dependent interactions between microbial species.</span></p><p><br></p>                    <br><br>
