Brown</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">. Accelerated mass loss of Himalayan glaciers since the Little Ice Age. </span><a href="https://www.nature.com/srep" target="_blank" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Scientific Reports</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">, 2021, Volume 11, Article number: 24284. </span><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1038/s41598-021-03805-8" target="_blank" style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">http://dx.doi.org/10.1038/s41598-021-03805-8</a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">. <span style="font-family: 宋体;">参与此项研究的除了利兹大学的研究人员之外，还有来自英国纽卡斯尔大学（</span>Newcastle University<span style="font-family: 宋体;">）以及邓迪大学（</span>University of Dundee<span style="font-family: 宋体;">）的研究人员。</span></span><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">该论文重建了小冰期</span>14798<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">条喜马拉雅冰川的大小和冰面。研究人员计算，冰川面积已经减少了</span>40%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，从峰值的</span>2.8<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">万</span>km<sup>2</sup><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">减少到今天的</span>1.96<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">万</span>km<sup>2</sup><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。在此期间，他们还失去了</span>390~586 km<sup>2</sup><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">的冰，这相当于今天欧洲中部阿尔卑斯山（</span>European Alps<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）、高加索（</span>Caucasus<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）和斯堪的纳维亚（</span>Scandinavia<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）地区的所有冰的总和。该团队计算出，融冰释放的水使全球海平面上升了</span>0.92~1.38 mm<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">通讯作者和利兹大学的地理学院（</span>University of Leeds School of Geography<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）副院长乔纳森·卡瑞维克（</span>Jonathan Carrivick<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）博士校说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“我们的研究结果清楚地表明</span>,<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">冰从喜马拉雅冰川的正在消融速度至少是过去几个世纪平均速度的十倍。这种消失速度的加速只是在过去几十年才出现的，并且与人类引起的气候变化相（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/climate+change/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">climate change</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）吻合。”</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">喜马拉雅山脉是世界上第三大冰川冰的家园，仅次于南极洲和北极，经常被称为“第三极（</span>'the Third Pole'<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）”。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">喜马拉雅冰川的加速融化，对依赖亚洲主要河流系统提供食物和能源的数亿人有着重大影响。这些河流包括雅鲁藏布江（</span>Brahmaputra<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）、恒河（</span>Ganges<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）和印度河（</span>Indus<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">该研究小组利用卫星图像和数字高程模型（</span>digital elevation models<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）绘制了</span>400-700<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">年前冰川的轮廓，并“重建”了冰川表面。卫星图像（</span></span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/satellite+images/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">satellite images</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）显示了标记着以前冰川边界的脊，研究人员利用这些脊的几何形状来估计以前的冰川范围和冰表面高度。比较冰川重建和现在的冰川，确定了小冰期和现在之间的体积和质量损失（</span><a href="https://phys.org/tags/mass+loss/" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">mass loss</span></a><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">）。</span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">喜马拉雅冰川总体上在尼泊尔东部和主要分水岭以北的不丹东部地区损失得更快。该研究表明，这种变化可能是由于山脉两侧的地理特征不同，以及它们与大气的相互作用导致了不同的天气模式（</span>weather patterns<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">喜玛拉雅冰川的消融速度也比陆地更快，因为湖泊会产生一些变暖效应。这些湖泊的数量和规模都在增加，因此可以预期，它们的质量损失将继续加速。同样，那些表面有大量自然碎片的冰川也在以更快的速度失去质量</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">虽然它们只占冰川总数的</span>7.5%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">，但却贡献了总体积损失的</span>46.5%<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">。</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">乔纳森·卡瑞维克博士说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“虽然我们必须立即采取行动，减少和减轻人为气候变化对冰川和融水河流的影响，但对冰川影响的建模也必须考虑到湖泊和碎片等因素的作用。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">邓迪大学地理和环境科学的高级讲师、上述论文的合作者西蒙·库克博士（</span>Dr. Simon Cook<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">）说</span>:<span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: 宋体;">“该地区的人们已经看到了几个世纪以来从未见过的变化。这项研究只是最新的证明，这些变化正在加速，它们将对整个国家和地区产生重大影响。”</span></span></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">上述介绍，仅供参考。欲了解更多信息，敬请注意浏览</span><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1038/s41598-021-03805-8" style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "arial black", "avant garde";">Abstract</span></a><br></p><p style="line-height: 2em;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: "times new roman";">Himalayan glaciers are undergoing rapid mass loss but rates of contemporary change lack long-term (centennial-scale) context. Here, we reconstruct the extent and surfaces of 14,798 Himalayan glaciers during the Little Ice Age (LIA), 400 to 700 years ago. We show that they have lost at least 40 % of their LIA area and between 390 and 586 km<sup>3</sup> of ice; 0.92 to 1.38 mm Sea Level Equivalent. The long-term rate of ice mass loss since the LIA has been between − 0.011 and − 0.020 m w.e./year, which is an order of magnitude lower than contemporary rates reported in the literature. Rates of mass loss depend on monsoon influence and orographic effects, with the fastest losses measured in East Nepal and in Bhutan north of the main divide. Locally, rates of loss were enhanced with the presence of surface debris cover (by 2 times vs clean-ice) and/or a proglacial lake (by 2.5 times vs land-terminating). The ten-fold acceleration in ice loss we have observed across the Himalaya far exceeds any centennial-scale rates of change that have been recorded elsewhere in the world.</span></p>                    <br><br>
