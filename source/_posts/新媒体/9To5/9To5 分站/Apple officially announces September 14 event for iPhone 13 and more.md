<h2 id="h-apple-september-event-is-official">Apple September event is official </h2>



<p>Apple‚Äôs September 14 event will be held at 10 a.m. PT/1 p.m. ET. This marks the second year in a row that Apple has held its annual September event completely virtually due to the COVID-19 pandemic. </p>



<p>The September 14 event will be live-streamed across Apple‚Äôs website, on the company‚Äôs YouTube channel, and via the Apple TV application on iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple TV. We‚Äôll have more details on how to watch when event day itself rolls around. </p>



<h2 id="h-what-to-expect">What to expect</h2>



<p>At this year‚Äôs September Apple event, we expect Apple to officially take the wraps off of the iPhone 13. The new iPhone is expected to bring a handful of new features. Rumors changes include a smaller notch, camera enhancements, ProMotion 120Hz display technology, and more. </p>



<p>The Apple Watch Series 7 is also likely to be announced during the September 14 event. With an all-new design and larger display, the Apple Watch Series 7 is shaping up to be a notable update. With that being said, however, reports indicate that Apple is facing production issues that could severely constrain supply. </p>



<p>Apple is also planning new AirPods that could be announced at the September event. AirPods 3 are rumored to feature a design similar to AirPods Pro, but without replaceable eartips.</p>



<p>Other possible hardware announcements include a new iPad mini and a new entry-level iPad. You should not, however, expect new Macs to be announced at the September event, with Apple likely saving those for a separate event in October or November. </p>



<p>Finally, we also expect Apple to announce the official release dates for its next round of software updates during the event. This includes iOS 15, watchOS 8, and tvOS 15. macOS Monterey, on the other hand, is likely not coming until sometime later this fall. </p>
