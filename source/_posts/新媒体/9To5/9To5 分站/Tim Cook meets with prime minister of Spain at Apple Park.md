<p>Apple CEO Tim Cook <a href="https://twitter.com/sanchezcastejon/status/1418654003840360448/photo/1">met</a> with Pedro Sánchez, the prime minister of Spain, at Apple Park in Cupertino this week. The two discussed the “digital transformation” among other topics during the meeting, according to <meta charset="utf-8">Sánchez. </p>
<p><span id="more-739658"></span>
</p>
<p>The prime minister <a href="https://twitter.com/sanchezcastejon/status/1418654003840360448/photo/1">took to Twitter</a> today to recap the meeting and share a photo, saying he had a productive meeting with the Apple CEO. The two reportedly discussed the Recovery Plan, which is Spain’s investment in their economy to help recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic. </p>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>Productive meeting with Tim Cook in San Francisco. Spain knows that digital transformation is a priority and the Recovery Plan offers us a unique opportunity to face this challenge. There is no better occasion for big technology companies like Apple to invest in our country.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>As reported by <a href="https://www.msnbc.com/morning-joe/watch/spanish-prime-minister-pedro-sanchez-travels-to-u-s-in-effort-to-promote-business-117122629881">MSBNC</a> earlier this week, Sánchez is in the US to promote Spanish businesses, address the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic and vaccinations, and more. <a href="https://english.elpais.com/spain/2021-07-22/on-us-trip-spanish-pm-seeks-to-soothe-investment-funds-touts-progress-of-covid-19-vaccination-campaign.html">Other reports have indicated</a> that he’s also met with executives from Bank of America, Morgan Stanley, and Blackstone. </p>
<p>Cook has regularly met with politicians during his tenure as Apple CEO, though the rate of those meetings  has slowed down amid the pandemic. </p>
