<p>
            Using Apple Photos and iCloud can fill up your MacBook's hard drive. So it's a good idea to transfer all of your pictures stored in Apple Photos and iCloud to Google Photos. This way, you can make them available on any device and reclaim the space on your Apple device.
    </p>

                        <p>
            After that, you can view, search, and sort them quickly without relying on iCloud. And you can even edit them using relevant native or third-party apps.
    </p>


    


                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            Here's how to export your pictures from Apple Photos and iCloud to Google Photos.
    </p>

                        <h2 id="how-to-move-apple-photos-to-google-photos-on-your-mac">
                    How to Move Apple Photos to Google Photos on Your Mac</h2>

                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            Apart from storing photos locally, the Apple Photos app lets you sync and <a href="https://www.makeuseof.com/tag/how-to-download-photos-from-icloud/">download pictures from your iCloud</a> account. You can download the original versions of your photos, but that tends to occupy more space on your Mac. So, most folks prefer using the optimized versions.
    </p>


    


                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            Transferring your pictures from your Mac to Google Photos will make them accessible from any device. If you have many pictures to move, Google's Backup and Sync tool can handle it efficiently.
    </p>


    


                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            Here's how to transfer your pictures from Apple Photos to Google Photos on your Mac:
    </p>


    


                
                    
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
            
                            <ol>
                    <li>
                        Open the <strong>Apple Photos</strong> app on Mac and go to <strong>Photos</strong> ><strong> Preferences</strong> in the menu bar on the top-left.
            </li>
                    <li>
                        Switch to the <strong>Cloud</strong> tab and enable the option for <strong>Download Originals on this Mac</strong> under iCloud Photos. You might have to wait for a bit if you plan to transfer the original versions of the photos.

            </li>
                    <li>
                        Download the <strong>Backup and Sync</strong> tool from the <a href="https://photos.google.com/apps" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">Google Photos apps</a> page.

                    <li>
                        Sign in to your Google account, and choose <strong>Back up photos and videos</strong> while setting up.
            </li>
                    <li>
                        The tool selects <strong>Pictures</strong> and <strong>Photos Library</strong> folders by default. Under that, choose <strong>High</strong> or <strong>Original</strong> upload quality.
            </li>
                    <li>
                        Hit <strong>Start</strong> to back up all your photos to Google Photos.

                        <p>
            If iCloud sync in the Apple Photos app is disabled, open <a href="https://www.icloud.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer">iCloud.com</a> on your Mac and download all the photos manually. Then, move those images to the Pictures folder on your Mac so that the Backup and Sync tool can save a copy in Google Photos.
    </p>


    


                
                    
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            After the sync finishes, you may remove the Backup and Sync tool. Also, keep in mind that deleting those pictures from your Mac won't remove them from Google Photos.
    </p>


    


                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            This method eliminates the need to keep a web browser running to directly upload several gigabytes of photos to the Google Photos site.
    </p>

                        <h2 id="how-to-transfer-icloud-photos-to-google-photos-on-iphone">
                    How to Transfer iCloud Photos to Google Photos on iPhone</h2>

                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            Moving pictures and videos is a bit easier if you have the Google Photos app installed on your iPhone. With that, you can directly transfer your photos without downloading them on your iPhone.
    </p>


    


                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            <strong>Download</strong>: Google Photos for <a href="https://apps.apple.com/app/google-photos/id962194608" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">iOS</a> (Free, in-app purchases available)
    </p>


    


                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            Here's how you can move images in iCloud Photos to Google Photos on your iPhone.
    </p>


    


                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                
            
            
                            <ol>
                    <li>
                        On iPhone, go to <strong>Settings</strong> ><strong> Photos</strong> and then select <strong>Download and Keep Originals</strong> (uses more storage) or <strong>Optimize iPhone Storage</strong>.
            </li>
                    <li>
                        Open the Google Photos app on your iPhone.
            </li>
                    <li>
                        Tap the <strong>Account</strong> icon in the upper-right corner and select <strong>Photo Settings</strong>.
            </li>
                    <li>
                        Select <strong>Back up & sync</strong> and enable the toggle for <strong>Back up & sync</strong>.
            </li>
                    <li>
                        Under Upload Size, you can choose between <strong>High Quality</strong> and <strong>Original</strong>.

                        <p>
            While Apple Photos and iCloud can simply store your pictures, Google Photos offers better tools to organize and sort them. After moving your photos, you can delete them from your Mac and even iCloud. That won't remove the pictures from your Google Photos account.
    </p>


    


                
                        
            
        
            
            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                
            
                                                                                                
                                
                                                



            
                        <p>
            Of course, all of this makes sense if you have enough Google storage space. If your Google Photos storage also fills up eventually, you can export those photos and videos. In this case, consider other cloud storage alternatives to Google Photos such as Amazon Photos, OneDrive, Dropbox, or Flickr.
    </p>
