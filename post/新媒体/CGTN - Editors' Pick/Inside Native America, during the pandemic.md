            <div class="text  en"><p>CGTN traveled to the Cheyenne River Sioux Native American reservation in South Dakota where tribal authorities are battling the coronavirus.</p><p>Native Americans have been hit hard by Covid-19. They’ve been twice as likely to die of the virus compared to white Americans.</p><p>The virus has triggered a cultural crisis, targeting some of the last speakers of the Native American languages – the elders.</p><p>But the reservation is fighting back to save their languages and keep people safe.</p><p>They're also vaccinating people at a quicker pace than the rest of the United States.</p><p><a href="https://america.cgtn.com/livestream/">Watch the series on CGTN </a>beginning March 22. </p><p><strong>Check out </strong><a href="https://mailchi.mp/6ffb4937f487/u3dwhefhos"><strong>The China Report</strong></a><strong>, our new weekly newsletter. Subscribe here!</strong></p></div>
