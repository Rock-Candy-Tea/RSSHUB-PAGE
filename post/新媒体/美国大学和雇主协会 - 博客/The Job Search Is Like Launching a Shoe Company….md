<p><span data-contrast="auto">Kenneth Cole </span><span data-contrast="auto">was able to succeed </span><span data-contrast="auto">in part </span><span data-contrast="auto">because he found a data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">In today's uncertain </span><span data-contrast="auto">employment </span><span data-contrast="auto">market, the same </span><span data-contrast="auto">s</span><span data-contrast="auto"> and abilities</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">More and more</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">As a result, e</span><span data-contrast="auto">ven when responding to postings that seem like a perfect fit with </span><span data-contrast="auto">ing </span><span data-contrast="auto">themselves short and</span><span data-contrast="auto"> not get</span><span data-contrast="auto">ting</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">the offer</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Here are </span><span data-contrast="auto">four</span><span data-contrast="auto"> ways </span><span data-contrast="auto">to help</span><span data-contrast="auto"> your students to showcase their full range of strengths in their applications and interviews.</span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><b><span data-contrast="auto">Do a </span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">Pre-Search </span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">Skills </span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">Inventory</span></b><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">It has been my experience that a lot of students begin their initial </span><span data-contrast="auto">career center </span><span data-contrast="auto">appointments with </span><span data-contrast="auto">statements or questions about their self-identified deficiencies as candidates (low GPA, lack of internships, little participation in extracurricular activities, etc.).</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">While self-awareness of areas needing improvement is important, it should always be accompanied by a knowledge of one’s strengths and skills—those unique factors that set candidates apart from others and are an integral part of their </span><span data-contrast="auto">b</span><span data-contrast="auto">rand.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">While there are </span><span data-contrast="auto">a number of</span><span data-contrast="auto"> formal assessment instruments that can assist students in skills identification, much can be learned through conversations.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">For example, in my first appointment with a student, I will often ask them to tell me why they decided on their </span><span data-contrast="auto">particular </span><span data-contrast="auto">m</span><span data-contrast="auto">ajor</span><span data-contrast="auto">, or what classes they enjoy the most.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">Generally, those things that students enjoy doing are things at which they are proficient—helping students to recognize their strengths and to encourage promoting these strengths in their communications with potential employers (even those </span><span data-contrast="auto">strengths that students initially dismiss as non-relevant) will </span><span data-contrast="auto">go a long towards ensuring that they are presenting a more complete candidate profile to recruiters.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><b><span data-contrast="auto">Read </span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">B</span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">etween the </span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">L</span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">ines</span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">.</span></b><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">Too many students </span><span data-contrast="auto">overly </span><span data-contrast="auto">focus on matching the qualifications and requirements </span><span data-contrast="auto">listed within job </span><span data-contrast="auto">postings and</span><span data-contrast="auto"> end up missing the implicit skills needed to succeed</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">A well-written posting will weave </span><span data-contrast="auto">both the essential hard and soft skills into the job description—and </span><span data-contrast="auto">both</span><span data-contrast="auto"> are equally important</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">Some postings make it easy to </span><span data-contrast="auto">identify</span><span data-contrast="auto"> these essential skills, others might take a bit of detective work</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Having students </span><span data-contrast="auto">highlight</span><span data-contrast="auto"> the active verbs within the </span><span data-contrast="auto">description</span><span data-contrast="auto"> can help</span><span data-contrast="auto"> with this process</span><span data-contrast="auto">—if they have experience in using any of those skills</span><span data-contrast="auto"> listed</span><span data-contrast="auto">, they should be highlighted in the cover letter and mentioned in interviews</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><b><span data-contrast="auto">Work on the Narrative</span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">.</span></b><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">Everyone’s career is a story, even students who are just beginning their journeys</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">And these career stories are </span><span data-contrast="auto">usually </span><span data-contrast="auto">not </span><span data-contrast="auto">merely statements of fact—they feature obstacles and setbacks to overcome, adaptations and adjustments made on the fly, and achieving hard-won goals.</span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Unfortunately, many s</span><span data-contrast="auto">tudents</span><span data-contrast="auto"> relate </span><span data-contrast="auto">accurate</span><span data-contrast="auto"> but bland </span><span data-contrast="auto">descriptions of their </span><span data-contrast="auto">experiences when answering interview questions and leave out those elements which </span><span data-contrast="auto">would have given the story more power and interest</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">For example, many students shy away from </span><span data-contrast="auto">talking about failures or setbacks in their interviews (unless specifically asked to </span><span data-contrast="auto">provide</span><span data-contrast="auto"> such an example)</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">This reluctance deprives students of opportunities to describe instances of resilience, and of lessons learned the hard way</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">This is </span><span data-contrast="auto">of course </span><span data-contrast="auto">not to say that every interview question will lend itself to a response full of drama—sometimes the simplest answer is the best one to give.</span><span> </span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><b><span data-contrast="auto">Practice, Practice, Practice</span></b><b><span data-contrast="auto">.</span></b><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">We all know or have seen people who are blessed with the gift of storytelling</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">Whether reciting a speech or just describing something that happened at work, t</span><span data-contrast="auto">he</span><span data-contrast="auto">ir</span><span data-contrast="auto"> words just seem to flow out in </span><span data-contrast="auto">a natural and unforced pace, and they seem completely comfortable throughout their narrations</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">It’s</span><span data-contrast="auto"> a shame that we </span><span data-contrast="auto">can’t</span><span data-contrast="auto"> all </span><span data-contrast="auto">possess</span><span data-contrast="auto"> this gift, but we can work on feeling more at ease in situations requiring storytelling</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">As many verbal interactions with employers </span><span data-contrast="auto">represent</span><span data-contrast="auto"> opportunities to use stories and examples to illustrate skills and strengths, </span><span data-contrast="auto">it’s</span><span data-contrast="auto"> important for students to become more accustomed to this process.</span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Too often, however, students mistake memorization for preparation and </span><span data-contrast="auto">as a consequence</span><span data-contrast="auto">,</span><span data-contrast="auto"> their elevator pitches and interview answers sound flat and…well, memorized</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">It can be difficult to break students of the memorization habit</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">As described earlier, having students talk in general about their passions and interests during a career center appointment (without trying to answer any of the standard practice interview questions) can help them feel more confident when articulating their strengths—subsequent </span><span data-contrast="auto">practice sessions employing more structured interview questions can then be used to help students answer these types of questions in a more natural style</span><span data-contrast="auto">, and be more willing to include both hard and soft skills in their responses (</span><span data-contrast="auto">and,</span><span data-contrast="auto"> in today’s virtual </span><span data-contrast="auto">recruiting and interviewing environment, naturalness is a plus).</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">A</span><span data-contrast="auto">t the same time</span><span data-contrast="auto">, </span><span data-contrast="auto">it’s</span><span data-contrast="auto"> important to stress to students that brief hesitancies or stumbling over words when talking to employers does not mean that their candidacy is doomed</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">Answers do not have to be perfectly recited and flawless to be considered great responses</span><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">What is more important is the ‘authenticity’ of the candidate’s answers—to what extent are their responses </span><span data-contrast="auto">truly reflective of who they are and what they have to offer?</span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span><span data-ccp-props="&#123;"201341983":0,"335559739":160,"335559740":259&#125;"> </span></p>
