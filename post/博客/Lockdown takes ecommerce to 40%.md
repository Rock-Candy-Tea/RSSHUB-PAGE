<div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-a5a9757eb9bcf3a3a156"><div class="sqs-block-content"><p class style="white-space:pre-wrap;">The growth of ecommerce has always fit the old line about a frog in boiling water. Every year, the share of retail taken by ecommerce rose a little higher, and physical retailers got a little more uncomfortable (and recently have started failing), but the growth in any given year was never dramatic enough to make headlines. That was until 2020, of course - now we’ve had a reset. Lockdowns triggered a huge spike in online sales of every kind, but then the question was where this would stabilise  - once things started to calm down, where would the new level be set? We’re now starting to see.</p><p class style="white-space:pre-wrap;">In the USA, where lockdowns have been pretty patchy, ecommerce was ~17% of addressable retail at the beginning of the year. It spiked up to 22.5% in Q2 and in Q3, reported last week, it dipped back to close to 20%. In the UK, where we have monthly data that makes the picture clearer, penetration was already 20%, spiked to over 30% and now seems to be stabilising in the high 20s. </p></div></div><div class="sqs-block gallery-block sqs-block-gallery" data-block-json="{"existingGallery":null,"hSize":null,"newWindow":false,"floatDir":null,"methodOption":"transient","aspectRatio":null,"auto-crop":true,"blockAnimation":"none","collectionId":"5fbaaf5360ed88028bda0e37","design":"stacked","show-meta":true,"show-meta-basic":true,"show-meta-only-title":false,"show-meta-only-description":false,"square-thumbs":true,"vSize":null,"transientGalleryId":"5fbaaf5360ed88028bda0e37"}" data-block-type="8" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1606066252120_21598"><div class="sqs-block-content">
