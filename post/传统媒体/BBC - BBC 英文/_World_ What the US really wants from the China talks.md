China is relegated to a stopover on the way back.</p></div></div><div data-component="text-block" class="ssrcss-uf6wea-RichTextComponentWrapper e1xue1i83"><div class="ssrcss-3z08n3-RichTextContainer e5tfeyi2"><p>And the meeting will be on American soil: that was extremely important, said a senior administration official.</p></div></div><div data-component="text-block" class="ssrcss-uf6wea-RichTextComponentWrapper e1xue1i83"><div class="ssrcss-3z08n3-RichTextContainer e5tfeyi2"><p>The idea is to counter the Chinese perception that the United States is on the decline, says Michael Green, the senior vice-president for Asia and Japan Chair at the Center for Strategic and International Studies.</p></div></div><div data-component="text-block" class="ssrcss-uf6wea-RichTextComponentWrapper e1xue1i83"><div class="ssrcss-3z08n3-RichTextContainer e5tfeyi2"><p>Chinese commentators like to say that the "winds are blowing to the East."
