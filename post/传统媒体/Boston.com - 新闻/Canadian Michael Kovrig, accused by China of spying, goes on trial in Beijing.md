that in dealing with the cases of Michael Kovrig and Michael Spavor, the United States will treat these two individuals as if they were American citizens," William Klein, chargé d'affaires of the U.S. The court said it will set a date later for a verdict.</p>  <p>Canadian and other diplomats were not allowed to attend Spavor's trial on what China said were national security grounds, a lack of transparency that Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau called "completely unacceptable."</p>  <p>Observers have said the likely convictions of the two men could ultimately facilitate a diplomatic agreement whereby they are released and sent back to Canada. as they have become known around the world. </p>  <p>But Nadjibulla said in an interview on CBC's <em><a href="https://gem.cbc.ca/season/rosemary-barton-live/season-1/37e41f88-63c3-4e45-85f3-b87e36849136?rbl-pareto-news">Rosemary Barton Live</a> that</em> she wants to see those words translated into actions that secure their release as soon as possible.</p>  <p>"Solidarity and support and words are good, and we must continue to say those things," Nadjibulla told host Rosemary Barton.</p>  <p>"But what really will make a difference for Michael [Kovrig] and for Michael Spavor now are actions and concerted diplomatic effort on the part of all three governments to find a path forward."</p>
