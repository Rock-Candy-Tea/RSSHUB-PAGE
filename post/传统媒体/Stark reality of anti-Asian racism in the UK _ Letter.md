<p>As a woman who is half Malaysian-Chinese and half white, I have spent my entire life being exoticised and stereotyped, writes <strong>Alex Smith</strong></p><p>After reading about the shocking and heartbreaking news of the Atlanta shootings (<a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2021/mar/18/fbi-pressure-anti-asian-hate-crime-atlanta" title>FBI under pressure to tackle anti-Asian hate crime in wake of Atlanta shootings</a>, 18 March), I can’t help but reflect on the prevalence of racism towards east and south-east Asians in our own country. The UK is rife with it. Covid-19 hasn’t caused the racism towards British east Asians and south-east Asians, as the UK media likes to claim.
