France-Presse — Getty Images</cite></figcaption></figure><div class="article-partial"><div class="article-body-item col-lg-5"><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">The Chinese government has signaled that it will soon begin trials of <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/12/09/world/asia/china-canada-michael-kovrig-spavor.html">two Canadian men</a> held in China for more than two years on vague charges of espionage, escalating a punitive campaign against Canada.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">中国政府已发出信号，将很快开庭审理两名已被中国关押了两年多的<a href="https://cn.nytimes.com/world/20201210/china-canada-michael-kovrig-spavor/">加拿大男子</a>，他们被含糊地指控从事间谍活动。这意味着中国对加拿大惩罚行动的升级。</div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">China plans to go forward with the trials, Canadian officials said, despite global pressure to release the two men, Michael Kovrig, a former diplomat, and Michael Spavor, a business consultant, who have been held largely in isolation since they were detained in 2018.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">这两人是前外交官康明凯(Michael Kovrig)和商业顾问迈克尔·斯帕沃尔(Michael Spavor)，自从2018年被拘留以来，他们基本上一直处于隔离关押中。加拿大官员说，尽管有要求释放这两人的全球压力，但中国打算按计划开庭审理他们的案件。</div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">The <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/06/19/world/asia/china-canada-kovrig-spavor.html">prosecution of the two men</a> is widely seen as retribution for Canada’s decision in 2018 to arrest <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2021/01/14/world/canada/canada-meng-huawei-detention.html">Meng Wanzhou</a>, a top executive of the Chinese technology giant Huawei, at the request of the United States. The start of the Mr. Spavor’s trial, on Friday, coincides with the <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2021/03/15/world/asia/blinken-japan-south-korea.html">first meeting</a> of senior American and Chinese officials since President Biden took office in January, <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2021/03/10/business/china-us-tech-rivalry.html">amid tensions</a> over technology, defense and other issues.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">人们普遍认为，<a href="https://cn.nytimes.com/china/20200619/china-canada-kovrig-spavor/" title="Link: https://cn.nytimes.com/china/20200619/china-canada-kovrig-spavor/">起诉这两人</a>是中国对<a href="https://cn.nytimes.com/world/20210115/canada-meng-huawei-detention/">孟晚舟</a>案的报复，2018年加拿大应美国要求逮捕了中国科技巨头华为的这名高管。对斯帕沃尔案的审理将于周五开始，这恰好是拜登总统今年1月上任以来美中两国高级官员首次会晤的日子，双方目前在技术、国防和其他问题上<a href="https://cn.nytimes.com/china/20210310/china-us-tech-rivalry/">关系紧张</a>。</div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Mr. Kovrig’s trial is to start Monday.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">对康明凯案的审理将于周一开始。<br></div></div></div></div></div><div class="article-partial"><div class="article-body-item col-lg-5"><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Here’s what to expect.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">下面是预计会发生的情况。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph"><b>Politics will hang over the trials.</b></div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph"><b>政治因素将影响案件审理。</b><br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Mr. Kovrig and Mr. Spavor have been caught up in a broader geopolitical struggle involving China, Canada and the United States.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">康明凯和斯帕沃尔被卷入了一场涉及中国、加拿大和美国的更广泛的地缘政治斗争。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">China has adopted an <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/04/17/world/asia/coronavirus-china-xi-jinping.html">increasingly aggressive</a> foreign policy in recent years, and experts say the detention of the two Canadians is part of a campaign by Beijing to show that it will not give in to demands from Western countries.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">近年来，中国采取了<a href="https://cn.nytimes.com/china/20200420/coronavirus-china-xi-jinping/">越来越咄咄逼人</a>的外交政策。专家说，拘留这两名加拿大人是北京展示它不会屈服于西方国家要求的运动的一部分。</div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Officials in Canada and the United States have accused China of holding the two Canadians as a bargaining chip to win the release of Ms. Meng, the daughter of Ren Zhengfei, the founder of Huawei and one of China’s most prominent businessmen.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">加拿大和美国官员指责中国扣押这两名加拿大人，将他们作为换取释放孟晚舟的谈判筹码。孟晚舟是中国最著名的商人之一、华为创始人任正非的女儿。</div></div></div><figure class="article-inline-photo large"><div class="img-box"><img src="https://static01.nyt.com/images/2021/03/18/world/18china-canada-2/merlin_185088465_9da6203c-a07c-4ef0-83cc-649a5111b632-master1050.jpg" alt="孟晚舟本周一在温哥华一家法院出庭。中国官员已多次呼吁加拿大释放孟晚舟。" data-src="https://static01.nyt.com/images/2021/03/18/world/18china-canada-2/merlin_185088465_9da6203c-a07c-4ef0-83cc-649a5111b632-master1050.jpg" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"></div><figcaption><span>孟晚舟本周一在温哥华一家法院出庭。中国官员已多次呼吁加拿大释放孟晚舟。</span> <cite>Darryl Dyck/The Canadian Press, via Associated Press</cite></figcaption></figure><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“We believe these detentions are arbitrary and remain deeply troubled by the lack of transparency surrounding these proceedings,” Marc Garneau, Canada’s minister of foreign affairs, said in a statement on Wednesday.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“我们认为，这些拘押是任意的，我们对有关法律程序缺乏透明度深感不安，”加拿大外交部长马克·加诺(Marc Garneau)在周三的一份声明中说。</div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Ms. Meng was arrested on sweeping fraud charges but was granted bail and has been living in a mansion in Vancouver.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">孟晚舟因涉嫌欺诈被捕，但她已获保释，并一直住在温哥华的一座豪宅里。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Chinese officials have repeatedly called on Canada to release Ms. Meng. China has described her arrest as an attempt by the United States to target China’s most successful companies and thwart its scientific and technological progress.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">中国官员多次呼吁加拿大释放孟晚舟。中国将她的被捕描述为美国打击中国最成功公司的行动，试图阻碍中国的科技进步。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“The Chinese judiciary handles cases independently according to the law and fully protects the legitimate rights of the persons involved,” Zhao Lijian, a spokesman for the Chinese foreign ministry, said at a regular news briefing in Beijing on Thursday. He did not provide details on the impending trials.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“中国司法机关依法独立办案，并充分保障有关人员各项合法权利，”中国外交部发言人赵立坚周四在北京举行的例行记者会上说。他对即将开始的审理没有提供任何细节。</div></div></div></div></div><div class="article-partial"><div class="article-body-item col-lg-5"><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph"><b>The proceedings will likely be shrouded in secrecy.</b></div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph"><b>审理很可能在保密中进行。</b></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">China maintains one of the most opaque legal systems in the world. Courts are controlled by the ruling Communist Party, and convictions in high-profile cases are almost certain, especially those involving national security.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">中国维持着世界上最不透明的法律体系之一。法院由执政的共产党控制，广受关注的案件几乎可以肯定会被定罪，尤其是涉及国家安全的案件。</div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">The trials will likely be shrouded in secrecy, with limited opportunities to examine evidence or hear rebuttals from the two men. The proceedings will likely be closed to the public and the news media.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">审理可能在保密状态下进行，外界复查证据或听取这两人反驳的机会有限。审理过程可能不对公众和新闻媒体开放。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Mr. Spavor’s family said in a statement on Thursday that Mr. Spavor had “very limited access” to defense lawyers during his time in detention.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">斯帕沃尔的家人在周四的一份声明中说，斯帕沃尔在被拘押期间与辩护律师的接触“非常有限”。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“The specific charges against Michael are vague and have not been publicly released,” Mr. Spavor’s family said. “He loved living and working in China and would never have done anything to offend the interests of China or the Chinese people. We stand by Michael and maintain his innocence in this difficult situation.”</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“针对迈克尔的具体指控很模糊，也没有公布出来，”斯帕沃尔的家人说。“他热爱在中国的生活和工作，绝不会做任何冒犯中国和中国人民利益的事情。在这种困难情况下，我们继续支持迈克尔，对他的清白坚信无疑。”</div></div></div><figure class="article-inline-photo large"><div class="img-box"><img src="https://static01.nyt.com/images/2021/03/18/world/18china-canada-3/merlin_183921864_f650e29d-9ede-4512-8fb1-40249aabc337-master1050.jpg" alt="加拿大驻北京大使馆。" data-src="https://static01.nyt.com/images/2021/03/18/world/18china-canada-3/merlin_183921864_f650e29d-9ede-4512-8fb1-40249aabc337-master1050.jpg" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"></div><figcaption><span>加拿大驻北京大使馆。</span> <cite>Mark Schiefelbein/Associated Press</cite></figcaption></figure><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Mr. Kovrig and Mr. Spavor are likely to be convicted and may receive harsh sentences, experts say, given the grave charges against them.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">专家说，考虑到康明凯和斯帕沃尔面临的严重指控，他们可能会被定罪，并可能受到重判。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“Nobody should be in any doubt that this is a completely political trial in both cases,” said Donald Clarke, a specialist in Chinese law at George Washington University. “If the merit of the cases had anything to do with it, we would not see them proceeding in absolute lock step in this way.”</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“任何人都不应该对这两起案件完全是政治审判有任何怀疑，”乔治华盛顿大学(George Washington University)的中国法律专家郭丹青(Donald Clarke)说。“如果这两个案件本身有任何有道理之处，我们就不会看到以这种绝对一致的方式审理他们。”</div></div></div></div></div><div class="article-partial"><div class="article-body-item col-lg-5"><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph"><b>Some are looking to the United States to help resolve the issue.</b></div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph"><b>有些人希望美国帮助解决这个问题。</b><br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">The issue of Ms. Meng and the “two Michaels,” as they are known in Canada, is likely to come up during the meetings between American and Chinese officials starting Thursday in Alaska, experts say, though a breakthrough is unlikely in the short term.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">专家说，孟晚舟和“两个迈克尔”（这是加拿大人对他们的叫法）的问题，可能会在美中官员周四于阿拉斯加开始的会晤中被提出来，不过短时间内不太可能有什么突破。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Mr. Biden has called for the release of the Canadians, saying last month that “human beings are not bartering chips.” But his administration has not indicated exactly how it might address the issue.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">拜登已呼吁释放这两名加拿大人，并在上个月表示，“人不是交换的砝码。”但他的政府还没有明确表示将如何解决这个问题。<br></div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Jerome A. Cohen, a New York University law professor who specializes in Chinese law, said the trials could pave the way for an agreement to secure the release of Mr. Kovrig and Mr. Spavor.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">专门研究中国法律的纽约大学(New York University)法学教授孔杰荣(Jerome A. Cohen)说，案件审理可能为达成让康明凯和斯帕沃尔获释的协议铺平道路。</div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“The P.R.C. is more likely to release them after vindicating its judicial sovereignty by convicting them,” Mr. Cohen said, referring to the People’s Republic of China.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“通过给他们定罪来证明本国的司法自主权后，中国更有可能释放他们，”孔杰荣说。</div></div></div><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">Friends and relatives of Mr. Kovrig and Mr. Spavor said it was important for Canada and the United States to take action to secure the release of the two men.</div></div><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">康明凯和斯帕沃尔的亲友说，加拿大和美国采取行动确保这两名男子获释非常重要。<br></div></div></div></div></div><div class="article-partial"><div class="article-body-item col-lg-5"><div class="row article-dual-body-item"><div class="col-lg-6"><br><div class="article-paragraph">“This is a stark reminder that real lives are on the line and that if we, Canada and the U.S., don’t intervene to secure their freedom, they will remain in great peril,” <a rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" href="https://www.ctvnews.ca/politics/we-can-do-both-kovrig-s-wife-disappointed-by-pm-citing-need-to-protect-canadians-amid-calls-for-meng-s-release-1.5002266">Vina Nadjibulla</a>, the wife of Mr. Kovrig, said in a telephone interview. “This is the moment.
