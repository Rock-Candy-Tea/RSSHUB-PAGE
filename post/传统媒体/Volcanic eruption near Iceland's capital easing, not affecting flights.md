More than 100,000 flights were grounded, stranding millions of passengers.</p>  <p>The Geldinga Valley eruption is the first on the Reykjanes Peninsula in almost 800 years.</p>  <div><span><figure class="imageMedia image full"><div class="placeholder"><div class="placeholderImage" style="padding-bottom:53.06%"></div><img loading="lazy" alt srcset="https://i.cbc.ca/1.5957782.1616252659!/fileImage/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_300/iceland-volcano-graphic-march-2021.jpg 300w,https://i.cbc.ca/1.5957782.1616252659!/fileImage/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_460/iceland-volcano-graphic-march-2021.jpg 460w,https://i.cbc.ca/1.5957782.1616252659!/fileImage/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_620/iceland-volcano-graphic-march-2021.jpg 620w,https://i.cbc.ca/1.5957782.1616252659!/fileImage/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_780/iceland-volcano-graphic-march-2021.jpg 780w,https://i.cbc.ca/1.5957782.1616252659!/fileImage/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_1180/iceland-volcano-graphic-march-2021.jpg 1180w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 300px,(max-width: 460px) 460px,(max-width: 620px) 620px,(max-width: 780px) 780px,(max-width: 1180px) 1180px" src="https://i.cbc.ca/1.5957782.1616252659!/fileImage/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/original_780/iceland-volcano-graphic-march-2021.jpg" referrerpolicy="no-referrer"></div></figure></span></div>  <p>The area began rumbling with increased seismic activity 15 months ago, and the tremors increased dramatically last month.</p>  <p>Over the past three weeks, the area has been rattled by about 50,000 small earthquakes, dozens of them magnitude 4 or stronger, the office said.</p>  <p>Iceland, located above a volcanic hot spot in the North Atlantic, averages one eruption every four to five years. The last one was at Holuhraun in 2014, when a fissure eruption spread lava the size of Manhattan over the interior highland region.</p>  <p>Scientists flew over the Geldinga Valley eruption on Saturday morning and estimated the eruptive fissure was about 500 metres long. The two streams of lava were about 2.5 kilometres from the nearest road.</p>  <div><span><ul class="similarLinks"><li class="similarListItem"><a href="https://www.cbc.ca/news/world/volcano-indonesia-eruption-1.5889399" class="similarLink" data-contentid flag text="Indonesian volcano unleashes river of lava in new eruption"><span class="similarLinkText">Indonesian volcano unleashes river of lava in new eruption</span></a></li></ul></span></div>  <p>Solny Palsdottir's house is the closest to the site of the eruption, just four kilometres away in the coastal town of Grindavik. She and her husband were watching TV on Friday night when her teenage son pointed out a red glow in the distance.</p>  <p>"Today, I see a white-blue cloud of steam coming from the mountains," Palsdottir, 50, told The Associated Press. "Not something I expected to have in my backyard.</p>  <p>"I am just relieved the earthquakes are over," she said.</p></span></div>
